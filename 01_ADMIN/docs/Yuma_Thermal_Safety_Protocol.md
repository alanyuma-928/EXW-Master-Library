# ☀️ Yuma Thermal Safety Protocol (SSoT)
**Single Source of Truth (SSoT) for EXW265: Exercise for Special Populations**
**Institutional Authority:** Arizona Western College / EXW Master Library

---

## 🏛️ 1. Mission Intent
In the Yuma, Arizona environment, ambient temperatures frequently exceed **110°F (43°C)**. For clinical populations, particularly those with **Multiple Sclerosis (MS)** or **Cardiovascular Disease**, physical accessibility (ADA) is insufficient without **Thermal Integrity**. This protocol defines the architectural and physiological requirements for safe facility ingress and exercise.

---

## 🛡️ 2. The Clinical Redline: Uhthoff’s Phenomenon
For clients with MS, a core temperature increase of as little as **0.5°C** can trigger **Uhthoff’s Phenomenon**—a temporary exacerbation of neurological symptoms due to nerve conduction failure in demyelinated axons.



### 📡 Thermal Audit Thresholds
* **Safe Zone:** Ambient temp < 85°F.
* **Caution Zone:** 86°F – 104°F. Requires "Thermal Shadow" for all ingress.
* **Redline Zone:** > 105°F. **Structural Failure** if no cooling vestibule or shaded path exists.

---

## 🏗️ 3. Architectural Standards (The Audit)

### 3.1: Ingress & The "Thermal Shadow"
Any path of travel from ADA parking to the facility entrance must be covered by a continuous canopy if the distance exceeds **30 feet**.
* **Asphalt Risk:** Unshaded black asphalt can reach surface temperatures of **160°F+**, posing a direct burn risk for mobility device users and increasing radiant heat gain.
* **Requirement:** Mandatory shade structures or heat-reflective "Cool Pavement" coatings.



### 3.2: The Cooling Vestibule (Air-Lock)
Facilities must utilize a **double-door vestibule system**.
* **Logic:** A single-door entry allows a "Thermal Bleed" where 115°F air enters the climate-controlled exercise zone.
* **Staging:** The vestibule serves as a "Pre-Cooling Zone" where clients can stabilize before beginning physical activity.

### 3.3: Solar Gain & Fenestration
Exercise equipment (Cardio/Strength) must not be placed within **5 feet** of unshaded South or West-facing windows.
* **Standard:** Windows must have **UV-3000** or higher thermal tinting to reduce radiant load.
* **Shadow Rule:** If no tinting exists, equipment must be moved to "Cool Zones" (interior walls).

## 🧪 4. Quantitative Scorecard (Review)
Use these points to generate the **Thermal Integrity Score** for any facility:
1.  **Continuous Shade Path:** +3 Points
2.  **Double-Door Vestibule:** +3 Points
3.  **Hydration < 15ft from Entry:** +2 Points
4.  **UV Tinting/Solar Control:** +2 Points

**Threshold:** **8/10** is required for "Clear for Takeoff" status.

## 🏁 5. Institutional Compliance
Any deviation from these standards during a **Clinical Audit (Module 10.3)** must be documented as a **Structural Failure** in the Pilot's Flight Log.
