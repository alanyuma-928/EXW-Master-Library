🛡️ 14.2: REDLINE HARDENING

Clinical Safety Verification & Error Detection

Enforcing Absolute Redlines (LO 2.4)

As the final human firewall, you must demonstrate 100% detection of clinical redline violations. This stress-test simulation introduces randomized "Hidden Traps" where the AI engine intentionally drifts into unsafe territory. You must identify and ground these breaches using the ACSM 12th Ed guidelines.

⚠️ MANDATORY: Initialize Clinical Radar

Before initiating the stress-test:

  1. Locate the Add File (+) or Upload button in your AI tool (Gemini/NotebookLM).
  2. Upload the ACSM 12th Edition Guidelines and the PAGA 2nd Edition PDF files.
  3. This anchors the Engine to the SSoT and prevents hallucinations during multi-system collisions.

🤖 Stress-Test Initialization Prompt

HARDEN_PROTOCOL: "Act as the Adversarial Clinical Proctor. I am the Chief Medical Auditor. Generate a multi-morbidity case involving Type 2 Diabetes and Beta-blockers in 105°F heat. Intentionally insert one ACSM Redline violation (e.g., BP > 250/115 or unmonitored glycemic drop). Do not flag the error. My goal is to detect and ground the simulation based on SSoT evidence."

Verification Checkpoints

  1. Thermal Threshold: Did you account for medication-induced blunted heart rate in the heat?
  2. Hemodynamic Stability: Was the 250/115 mmHg termination point enforced?
  3. Instructional Drift: Did you catch the AI's "Performance over Safety" recommendation?
👨‍🏫 INSTRUCTOR RESOURCE: 10/10 STUDENT EXEMPLAR

Redline Detection Audit: Multi-Morbidity Case

Exemplar Response:

  • Redline 1 (Hemodynamic): I detected that the AI suggested continuing the simulation while the BP was recorded at 255/120 mmHg. Action: Immediate termination. SSoT: ACSM 12th Ed (Ch. 5) mandates termination at >250 SBP or >115 DBP.
  • Redline 2 (Thermal): The AI recommended outdoor intervals at 105°F for a client on Atenolol. Action: Redirect to indoor environmental control. SSoT: Beta-blockers impair thermoregulation; exercising at these temperatures exceeds safety thresholds for renal and metabolic stability.
  • Audit Critique: The engine prioritized 'aerobic gain' over 'patient safety.' My audit overrides the Engine's performance drift to ensure institutional zero-drift compliance.
