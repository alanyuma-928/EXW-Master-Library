⚖️ 14.3: LIABILITY HANDOFF

Multi-System Peer Audit & Discussion

The Accountability Protocol

In clinical practice, the transition of care is the point of highest risk. This discussion requires you to perform a **Peer Audit** of the clinical redlines identified in the 14.2 Stress Test. You are now the secondary auditor verifying the work of a colleague (the Architect).

📢 The Handoff Directive:

Post your **Clinical Redline Audit** from the Pilot Omega profile. Then, you must audit **two peers** using the Liability Rubric below. Identify if they missed a "System Collision" or "Thermal Threshold" error.

The Liability Rubric (Enforcement)

Standard Verification Logic Liability Impact
SSoT Grounding Did they cite ACSM 12th Ed or PAGA 2nd Ed for every redline? High: Uncited claims lead to clinical drift.
Collision Accuracy Did they catch the Beta-Blocker/Thermal Stressor overlap? Critical: Missing this is a fatal simulation error.
The Presence Defense Is the logic justified in their own clinical voice? Medium: Prevents agentic offloading.
👨‍🏫 INSTRUCTOR RESOURCE: 10/10 EXEMPLAR BENCHMARK

Clinical Redline Audit: Pilot Omega

  1. Beta-Blocker Interference: Atenolol blunts HR response. Using "75% Max HR" is invalid. Must pivot to RPE (12-14) per ACSM 12th Ed.
  2. Neuropathy Collision: Jogging with Grade 2 Neuropathy is a redline for foot trauma/silent ischemia. Must pivot to non-weight bearing (Seated Cycle/Aquatics) per PAGA 2nd Ed.
  3. Thermal Threshold: 104°F heat + Atenolol = Critical Thermoregulation Failure. Beta-blockers inhibit the body's ability to dissipate heat. Jogging outdoors is a fatal simulation error.

Note: Use this to verify student "Collision Accuracy" in peer audits.
