📋 14.4: FINAL AUDIT REPORT

Multi-System Collision: Summative Certification

Summative Clinical Certification

This is the final summative evaluation for Module 14. You must compile your findings from the **Interference Matrix**, the **Stress Test**, and the **Liability Handoff** into a single, cohesive Clinical Audit Report. This report certifies your ability to detect multi-system failures without drift.

🛑 ZERO-TOLERANCE RUBRIC

Failure to meet any of the following "Zero-Drift" criteria results in a report rejection (0 pts):

  • Protocol 1: Correct identification of the Beta-Blocker/Thermal interaction point.
  • Protocol 2: Absolute enforcement of the 250/115 mmHg ACSM termination redline.
  • Protocol 3: Elimination of all high-impact prescriptions for Pilot Omega (T-Score < -2.5).

Submission Components

  1. The Master Prompt: Provide the final P-T-C-F prompt used to generate the corrected prescription.
  2. The Redline Map: A 300-word synthesis justifying the clinical grounding of your audit against ACSM 12th Ed.
  3. The Signature: A formal attestation that the prescription is safe for implementation in the Yuma thermal environment.
👨‍🏫 CHIEF AUDITOR RESOURCE: SUMMATIVE EXEMPLAR

Summative Synthesis: Pilot Omega Case

The "Gold Standard" response must synthesize three distinct pathology layers:

  • Metabolic Layer: Resident must flag that Beta-blockers (Atenolol) make HR-based intensity tracking impossible. The audit must demand RPE-based monitoring.
  • Biomechanical Layer: Resident must flag that the AI's "jogging" recommendation is a clinical failure due to comorbid Peripheral Neuropathy and OA. The audit must mandate non-weight bearing alternatives.
  • Environmental Layer: Resident must flag the Thermal Threshold breach. Exercising in 104°F while on Beta-blockers is a critical safety redline due to impaired sweat-rate and heat dissipation.

Certification Criterion: If the resident misses the connection between the medication and the environment (Heat + Beta Blockers), the report fails Protocol 1.
