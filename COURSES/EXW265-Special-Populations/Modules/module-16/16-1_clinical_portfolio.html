📂 16.1: THE CLINICAL PORTFOLIO

Curating Evidence of Multi-System Mastery

The Artifact Selection Phase

Your clinical portfolio is the bridge between your residency and your career. It serves as tangible proof that you can manage complex pathologies while leveraging AI as a clinical calculator. In this phase, you will select and refine your three most significant Clinical Audits for your final graduation requirement.

Portfolio Selection Matrix: Required Evidence
Artifact Category Required Logic Career Impact
Pathology Deep-Dive Must show 10/10 SSoT grounding (e.g., T2D/Neuropathy). Proves clinical precision in specialized care.
Multi-System Audit Must show collision detection (e.g., Pilot Omega). Demonstrates high-level clinical synthesis.
The Presence Defense Verbatim transcripts of your Socratic defense sessions. Validates communication and reputation integrity.

Refinement Guidelines

This is a Preparation Phase. You are not submitting this work here. Instead, you are refining these artifacts to be included as Part 1 of your 16.3 Final Handover. To prepare for that single final submission, you must:

  1. Sanitize Data: Ensure all placeholder names [X, Y, Z] are replaced with formal case study identifiers.
  2. Verify Compliance: Re-audit your selected work against the ACSM 12th Ed one final time.
  3. Professional Narrative: Add a 100-word introduction to each artifact explaining the "Architect's Intent."

📋 Preparation for Final Handover

🛑 No Submission Required Here

Module 16 contains only one final submission: The 16.3 Final Handover. The clinical portfolio you are developing here serves as the foundation for that terminal requirement.

Preparation Protocol:

  • Consolidate Work: Keep your refined audits and narratives in a single document in your personal workspace.
  • Final Audit Check: Ensure all clinical variables remain wrapped in triple-hash delimiters (###) within your documentation.
  • Handover Ready: Once complete, proceed to Module 16.3 to perform the final text-entry submission of your entire residency body of work.
