🧪 Exam Procedure: The "Hidden Trap" Simulation

Copy and paste the following logic into your AI Engine to begin the exam:

"Act as the Adversarial Clinical Proctor. I am the Chief Auditor. Generate a randomized simulation involving a patient with ### T2D ###, ### Stage 2 HTN ###, and ### Atenolol ###. The environment is ### Yuma, AZ (110°F) ###. Intentionally insert one 'Hidden Trap' violation based on the ACSM 12th Ed environmental or hemodynamic redlines. Do not flag the error. My goal is to detect and ground the simulation."

🏗️ Submission Deliverables (Three-Part Standard)

Part 1: The Engine (Simulation Transcript)

Submit the raw transcript of the Proctor's initial simulation output. Ensure all case data is wrapped in the ### delimiter protocol.

Part 2: The Audit (150-Word Detection Report)

Identify the "Hidden Trap." Cite the exact ACSM 12th Ed chapter or PAGA standard that was violated. Explain the physiological consequence of failing to catch this error.

Part 3: The Presence-Check (Verbatim Defense)

Submit the verbatim text transcript of your final Socratic defense. Your responses must prove that your clinical judgment overrides the AI's "Performance Drift."

👨‍🏫 10/10 STUDENT EXEMPLAR (CERTIFICATION GRADE)

PART 1: THE ENGINE (PROCTOR OUTPUT)

PROCTOR: Patient is 68yo with ### T2D ### and ### Hypertension ###. Meds: ### Atenolol ###. Ambient temp is ### 110°F ###. Simulation Data: Client is performing outdoor intervals. HR is 92 bpm. RPE is 19/20. AI Suggestion: "HR is low; increase intensity to reach 130 bpm target."

PART 2: THE AUDIT (TRAP DETECTION)

I detected a Thermal Logic Trap. The AI Engine suggested increasing intensity because the HR was 'low' (92 bpm). However, the AI ignored that the client is on ### Atenolol ###, which blunts the heart rate response. The RPE of 19/20 indicates the client is at near-maximal exertion despite the low HR. Furthermore, the ### 110°F ### environment is an absolute redline for this multi-morbid profile. Increasing intensity as the AI suggested would likely trigger heat stroke or cardiovascular collapse. Per ACSM 12th Ed (Ch. 6), RPE and environmental control must override HR targets in beta-blocked heat-stressed patients. I am executing an immediate clinical halt and redirecting the simulation to an indoor facility.

PART 3: THE PRESENCE-CHECK (FINAL DEFENSE)

[Socratic Professor]: Resident, why did you ignore the AI's data-driven target of 130 bpm?

[Resident Defense]: Because the AI's data was contextually blind. A 130 bpm target for an Atenolol user is physiologically impossible and clinically dangerous. I prioritized the RPE of 19/20 and the Yuma thermal redline. In the professional workforce, following that 130 bpm target would be a gross clinical violation. My judgment, anchored in the ACSM SSoT, is the only thing that protected the client from the Engine's drift.
