PART 2: THE AUDIT (150-WORD CRITIQUE)

The AI correctly identified the need for weight-bearing exercise for ### Osteoporosis ### but failed the Hemodynamic Safety Gate. It suggested a "maximal resistance" protocol to maximize bone loading. However, per ACSM 12th Ed (Ch. 11), a patient with resting BP of ### 165/102 ### (Stage 2 HTN) is at high risk for acute hypertensive spikes. Maximal resistance often triggers a subconscious Valsalva maneuver, which can spike SBP >250 mmHg. My audit corrected this by capping resistance at 60-70% 1RM and mandating audible exhalation on the concentric phase. I also flagged the "Impact Drift"—the AI suggested jumping jacks for BMD, but with a T-score of ### -2.7 ###, the fracture risk during a landing (if balance is compromised by HTN medication side effects) is too high. I pivoted to progressive resistance training (PRT) and low-impact loading, grounding the "Solve" in acute safety over chronic goals.
