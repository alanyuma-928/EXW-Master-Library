9.3: Osteoporosis & Bone Loading

Learning Objective 2.2: Fracture-Safe Prescription

Clinical Context: Osteoporosis

For clients with low bone density, the exercise goal is to provide enough mechanical load to stimulate osteoblast activity without exceeding the bone's structural limits. According to PAGA 2018, weight-bearing and resistance training are essential pillars of bone health.

🚫 CRITICAL SAFETY GATE: NO WEIGHTED SPINAL FLEXION

To prevent vertebral compression fractures, strictly avoid exercises that combine spinal flexion with external loads (e.g., weighted crunches, toe touches). Maintain a neutral spine during all resistance movements.

Table 9.3: Bone-Loading Safety Standards (PAGA 2018)
Protocol Modality Clinical Safety Redline
Weight-Bearing Walking, stair climbing, tennis. Avoid high-impact/explosive landings or falls.
Resistance 8-12 reps, moderate intensity. MANDATORY: Neutral spine axial loading only.
