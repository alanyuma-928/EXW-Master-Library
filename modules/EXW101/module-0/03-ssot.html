🏗️ Navigating the Evidence

In this residency, we do not rely on "vibes" or "common knowledge." We rely on the Single Source of Truth (SSoT). For all exercise prescriptions, your primary authority is:

Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans (PAGA)

2nd Edition (2018)

United States Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)

✅ P-T-C-F Engine Checklist

Before you "Launch" your prompt, verify it against these four flight controls:

  • 🟢 Persona: Did you tell the AI who to be? (e.g., "Act as a Dietitian").
  • 🔵 Task: Is your command specific? (e.g., "Analyze this menu").
  • 🟠 Context: Did you include the clinical environment? (e.g., "Yuma heat").
  • 🟣 Format: Did you request a specific layout? (e.g., "A numbered list").

🔗 Advanced Maneuver: Prompt Chaining

Do not ask the AI to do everything at once. Use Prompt Chaining to ensure clinical safety:

  1. Step 1: Ask the AI to define the PAGA/ACSM standards for your population.
  2. Step 2: Ask the AI to draft the plan based on the standards it just defined.
  3. Step 3: Ask the AI to audit its own plan for safety hallucinations.

🧱 The Structural Delimiter

Use delimiters to keep the AI "Engine" organized. When providing large datasets or specific guidelines, wrap them in clear markers:

### BEGIN SOURCE OF TRUTH ###
[Insert PAGA or ACSM Text Here]
### END SOURCE OF TRUTH ###

🧪 Practice Challenge

Try your first "Centaur" maneuver. Copy the following prompt into Gemini and observe the output:

"Act as a Clinical Exercise Architect. Using the PAGA 2018 guidelines, explain the importance of 150 minutes of moderate activity for a student in Yuma, Arizona. Provide the answer in a professional bulleted list."

The Clinical Audit Requirement

Because AI can "hallucinate," every assignment in this course requires a two-part submission:

  1. The Engine Output: The raw text generated by your AI prompt.
  2. The 150-Word Clinical Audit: A human-led critique where you verify the AI's claims against the **PAGA 2018** standards.
