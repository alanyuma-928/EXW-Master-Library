# ACSM's Guidelines for Exercise Testing & Prescription (12th Ed)
## Secondary SSoT for EXW265: Exercise for Special Populations

This document extracts the ACSM pre-participation and clearance algorithms specifically governing safety bounds for Special Populations (individuals managing chronic or identified conditions).

### The Special Populations Identifier

Under ACSM protocols, an individual is immediately flagged for special clinical handling if they are carrying a **Known Disease**:
* **Cardiovascular Disease (CVD)**: Cardiac, peripheral vascular, or cerebrovascular disease.
* **Metabolic Disease**: Type 1 or Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus.
* **Renal Disease**: Chronic kidney disease.

### Medical Clearance Rules for Special Populations

The following clearance mechanisms dictate the required medical gateways for Special Populations before initiating or increasing an exercise prescription:

###
**The Sedentary Population (Not Currently Active)**
* **Known Disease + Asymptomatic**: Medical clearance **IS** recommended before beginning exercise. Following clearance, the patient must start with light-to-moderate intensity.
* **Any Symptoms**: Medical clearance **IS** recommended.
###

###
**The Active Population (Currently Active)**
* **Known Disease + Asymptomatic**: Medical clearance is **NOT** necessary to continue moderate-intensity activities. However, medical clearance **IS** recommended before progressing to vigorous-intensity activities.
* **Any Symptoms**: **DISCONTINUE** exercise immediately and seek medical clearance.
###

### Clinical Safety Rules & The Redlines

When working with Special Populations, practitioners must vigilantly monitor for absolute failure events or emerging ischemic indicators.

###
**Mandatory Stop & Clearance Triggers (Major Signs or Symptoms)**
The emergence of any of the following "Redlines" overrides all prior clearances, triggering an immediate stop and mandatory medical review:
1. **Pain/Discomfort**: In the chest, neck, jaw, or arms suggestive of myocardial ischemia.
2. **Dyspnea**: Shortness of breath at rest or with mild exertion.
3. **Syncope**: Fainting or dizziness, especially during exercise.
4. **Orthopnea**: Dyspnea when lying flat.
5. **Ankle Edema**: Swelling in the lower extremities.
6. **Palpitations/Tachycardia**: Forcible or irregular heartbeat.
7. **Intermittent Claudication**: Pain in the lower limbs due to inadequate blood supply.
8. **Known Heart Murmur**.
9. **Unusual Fatigue** with usual activities.
###

###
**GY 116 Clinical Termination Criteria**
During any exercise testing within the GY 116 indoor lab, testing for special populations must be terminated immediately if any of the following Absolute Indications manifest:
* Drop in SBP > 10 mmHg with increased work rate.
* Moderate-to-severe Angina (3 on a 4-point scale).
* Increasing nervous system symptoms (ataxia, dizziness, near-syncope).
* Signs of poor perfusion (cyanosis or pallor).
* Technical difficulties in monitoring ECG or SBP.
* The subject requests to stop.
###
