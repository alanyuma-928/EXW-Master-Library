# Dietary Guidelines for Americans (DGA 2025-2030)
## Core Guidelines for EXW150: Nutrition

### Foundation: "Eat Real Food"
These Guidelines mark a significant shift, emphasizing real, whole foods over highly processed alternatives. The core mission is to return to basics to combat chronic diseases driven by the Standard American Diet.
* Prioritize diets built on whole, nutrient-dense foods: protein, dairy, vegetables, fruits, healthy fats, and whole grains.
* Dramatically reduce highly processed foods laden with refined carbohydrates, added sugars, excess sodium, unhealthy fats, and chemical additives.

### Core Nutritional Pillars

#### 1. Prioritize Protein Foods at Every Meal
* Consume a variety of animal-sourced (eggs, poultry, seafood, red meat) and plant-sourced (beans, lentils, nuts, soy) proteins.
* **Goal**: 1.2–1.6 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day.
* **Preparation**: Swap deep-fried foods for baked, broiled, roasted, stir-fried, or grilled methods.

#### 2. Dairy & Gut Health
* Consume full-fat dairy with no added sugars.
* **Dairy Goal**: 3 servings per day (for a 2,000-calorie pattern).
* Support gut health and the microbiome by eating fermented foods (e.g., sauerkraut, kimchi, kefir, miso) and high-fiber foods.

#### 3. Vegetables, Fruits, & Whole Grains (Goal for 2,000 kcal)
* **Vegetables**: 3 servings per day.
* **Fruits**: 2 servings per day. Limit 100% juice; prioritize whole foods.
* **Whole Grains**: 2–4 servings per day. Significantly reduce refined carbohydrates like white bread and packaged crackers.

#### 4. Incorporate Healthy Fats
* Eat healthy fats from meats, eggs, omega-3 rich seafood, avocados, nuts, and olives.
* When cooking, prioritize olive oil, butter, or beef tallow.
* Limit saturated fat consumption to no more than 10% of total daily calories.

#### 5. Limit Highly Processed Foods, Added Sugars, & Refined Carbohydrates
* Avoid sugar-sweetened beverages, artificial flavors, petroleum-based dyes, and non-nutritive sweeteners (e.g., aspartame, sucralose).
* Avoid "invisible" sugars ending in "-ose" or listed as syrups.
* **Strict Sugar Limit**: One meal should contain **no more than 10 grams** of added sugars.
* Grain snacks should not exceed 5g added sugar per ¾ ounce; dairy snacks should not exceed 2.5g added sugar per ⅔ cup.

#### 6. Sodium & Beverage Protocol
* **Sodium Limit**: Less than 2,300 mg per day (ages 14+). *Note: Highly active individuals may require more to offset sweat losses.*
* Prioritize water (still or sparkling) and unsweetened beverages.

### Special Population Considerations

* **Infancy & Early Childhood (Birth–4 Years)**: Feed only breast milk or iron-fortified formula for ~6 months. No added sugars. Introduce early allergens (like peanuts) and complementary foods around 6 months.
* **Adolescence (11–18 Years)**: Heightened need for energy, protein, calcium, and iron (especially for girls). Significantly limit energy and sugary drinks.
* **Pregnant & Lactating Women**: Increased need for folate, iron, iodine, and choline (eggs). Consume low-mercury omega-3 seafood (salmon, sardines).
* **Older Adults**: Lower caloric needs but equal or greater micronutrient needs (Protein, B12, D, Calcium).
* **Individuals with Chronic Disease**: A lower carbohydrate diet may improve health outcomes for certain conditions (e.g., Type 2 Diabetes).
* **Vegetarians & Vegans**: High vulnerability to nutrient gaps (Vitamins A, D, E, B6, B12, Calcium, Iron, Choline, Zinc). Requires diversified plant proteins and targeted supplementation.
