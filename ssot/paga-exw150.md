# Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans (PAGA 2018)
## Secondary SSoT for EXW150: Energy Balance & Weight Management

While the Dietary Guidelines for Americans (DGA) outline primary nutritional protocols, the Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans (PAGA 2018) establish the secondary framework required for calculating energy balance, facilitating weight loss, and improving cardiometabolic health.

### 1. Energy Balance & Weight Maintenance
* **The Interdependence Rule**: Physical activity and caloric intake must both be considered when attempting to control body weight. Physical activity is a critical lever in creating the energy deficit required for weight loss.
* **Maintenance Threshold**: While 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week yields broad health benefits, many individuals require **more than 150 minutes** to actively offset caloric intake and maintain a stable weight over time. 

### 2. Clinical Weight Loss & Rebound Prevention
* **The 300-Minute Marker**: Individuals seeking to lose a substantial amount of weight (greater than 5% of their total body weight), or those attempting to prevent weight regain after significant loss, typically need to achieve **more than 300 minutes** of moderate-intensity activity a week.
* **Synergistic Approach**: Strong scientific evidence confirms that combining both caloric restriction and physical activity is substantially more beneficial for achieving and maintaining weight loss than relying on either caloric restriction or physical activity alone.

### 3. Body Composition & Lean Mass Support
* **Preserving Muscle**: Muscle-strengthening activities must be integrated to help maintain lean body mass during weight loss phases. Though aerobic activity burns more immediate calories, strength training helps promote long-term weight maintenance.
* **Childhood Adiposity**: For youth ages 3 through 17 years, higher levels of physical activity are deeply correlated with smaller increases in body weight and adiposity throughout development.

### 4. Cardiometabolic Health & Obesity Support
* **Type 2 Diabetes Defense**: Regular physical activity dramatically reduces the risk of developing type 2 diabetes—an effect that scales multiplicatively because activity prevents excessive weight gain (which is an independent risk factor for the disease).
* **Equitable Benefits**: Individuals currently classified as overweight or obese obtain the exact same fundamental health benefits from physical activity as individuals at normal weights. 
* **HIIT Protocols**: High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) often provides greater cardiovascular benefits and insulin sensitivity improvements for adults with overweight/obesity or Type 2 Diabetes compared to standard continuous moderate-intensity exercise.
