The Einstein Protocol & Clinical AI Residency Framework

📐 1. The Architecture (High-Friction Learning)

In the Matador Wellness Center, we actively resist "Agentic Offloading"—the tendency for students to let AI perform the thinking rather than the processing. Our architecture forces human presence through intentional "High-Friction" design.

The Einstein Defense

Every assessment is designed to prevent "one-and-done" prompting. Students must provide evidence of their own voice and clinical judgment through audio/video defenses.

Localized Variables

Grounding content in the Yuma, AZ / Imperial Valley, CA region ensures that generic web-scraping agents cannot fulfill clinical requirements without understanding local demographics and environmental heat protocols.

🏛️ 2. The Guided Learning Trifecta

Students master three distinct roles to ensure human judgment remains the primary driver of clinical care.

ROLE 1

The Architect

Building precise clinical instructions (P-T-C-F) that "lock" the AI into a professional persona and specific environmental context.

ROLE 2

The Pilot

Maintaining situational awareness and providing the clinical judgment necessary to steer the AI engine safely.

ROLE 3

The Auditor

Performing mandatory 150-word clinical audits against the Source of Truth (PAGA/ACSM) to eliminate hallucinations.

🚀 3. The AdaptiveFit Assistant

Deploy custom Google Gemini assistants designed to automate routine clinical data processing while you maintain total architectural control.

  • Clinical Calculator: Rapid processing of VO2max, METs, and Macronutrient ratios.
  • Engine Room: A dedicated workspace for stress-testing exercise prescriptions.
  • SSoT Guardrails: Pre-programmed compliance with PAGA 2nd Edition.
