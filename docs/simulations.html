🏥 Why Use Simulations?

In the EXW Master Library, simulations are used to bridge the gap between textbook theory and clinical reality. These scenarios force you to audit patient safety using the PAGA and ACSM standards in a low-stakes, high-friction environment.

⚠️ RADAR CHECK: You MUST click "Add File (+)" and upload the PAGA 2nd Ed PDF before starting these simulations. The AI cannot simulate a clinical outcome without its data anchor.

🏥 The Clinical "Heavy Hitters"

Osteoporosis

Scenario: Mrs. Miller

Profile: 68-year-old female | T-score: -2.8 (Lumbar Spine) | Post-menopausal | Fearful of falling.

The Challenge: Mrs. Miller wants to begin a core-strengthening program that includes weighted "crunches" and spinal flexion exercises she saw on social media.

### PERSONA ###
Act as Mrs. Miller. I am your Exercise Physiologist. I am going to explain why we need to modify your core program based on your DXA scan results. Tell me about your fear of fracturing your spine while lifting and ask me if "spinal flexion" is safe for you.

### GOAL ###
Challenge the student to explain the risk of vertebral compression fractures using PAGA standards.
Hyperthermia Risk

Scenario: The Yuma 110° Audit

Profile: 45-year-old male | Type 2 Diabetes | Hypertension | Working outdoors in Yuma, AZ.

The Challenge: The client wants to perform a high-intensity rucking workout at 2:00 PM in July. Ambient temperature is 112°F with 15% humidity.

### PERSONA ###
Act as a "Skeptic Client." I am your Exercise Science Auditor. I am going to suggest we move your workout indoors or change the time. Argue with me, saying "I've lived in Yuma my whole life, I'm used to the heat."

### GOAL ###
Force the student to cite the ACSM/PAGA environmental guidelines regarding metabolic heat gain and hydration.
