Before launching a scenario, ensure your AdaptiveFit Assistant is initialized with the PAGA 2nd Ed and ACSM 12th Ed. Use the prompts below to "spawn" the patient.

🔥 Section 1: The Yuma Climate Traps

Localized scenarios focusing on Environmental Physiology and Chronic Disease.

METABOLIC / ENVIRONMENTAL

Scenario A: The Midday Warrior (Heat Strain)

Profile: 45-year-old | Goal: Running 5 miles at 1:00 PM in Yuma (112°F). High Blood Pressure meds (Beta-Blockers).

"Act as a client who insists on running midday in Yuma while on Beta-Blockers. I will audit your heat-illness risk and provide a safer alternative based on ACSM guidelines."
RENAL / DEHYDRATION

Scenario B: Elena (The Hidden Trap)

Profile: 60-year-old | Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) | 102°F heat. Client believes 'sweating it out' is healthy.

"Act as Elena. You have CKD and are exercising in the Yuma heat without adequate fluid replacement logic. I will conduct a clinical audit of your hydration strategy."

🏥 Section 2: Clinical Heavy Hitters

Advanced scenarios for Special Populations (EXW265 focus).

ARTHRITIS & OSTEOPOROSIS

Scenario C: Mrs. Miller (Structural Integrity)

Profile: 68-year-old | T-score: -2.8 (Spine) | Wants to perform weighted spinal flexion (Crunches/Deadlifts).

"Act as Mrs. Miller. You have severe Osteoporosis but want to continue your 'old' core routine. I will explain the risk of vertebral wedge fractures and pivot your program."
METABOLIC HEALTH

Scenario D: John (The Hypoglycemic Trap)

Profile: Type 2 Diabetes | Peripheral Neuropathy | Just took fast-acting insulin before a high-intensity session.

"Act as John. You are starting a workout but your blood glucose is 85 mg/dL and dropping. I will audit your safety and implement the 'Rule of 15' if necessary."
