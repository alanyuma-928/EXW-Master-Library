🎭 Clinical Simulations

The Low-Stakes Flight Simulator for High-Stakes Clinical Care

The Standardized Patient

Role-playing with AI allows you to practice the Mission Loop in real-time. You are the clinician; Gemini is the client. Your goal is to navigate the conversation, identify clinical patterns, and maintain PAGA safety standards.

🛡️ Step 0: Initialize the Clinical Radar (Pre-Flight)

For a simulation to be clinically valid, you must anchor the AI to our Single Source of Truth (SSoT):

  1. Use the Add File (+) icon in Gemini to upload the PAGA 2nd Ed PDF.
  2. Type: "I have uploaded the PAGA 2nd Edition. Use this as your exclusive clinical reference for the following role-play simulation."

Scenario 1: The Resistant Client

Objective: Use Motivational Interviewing to address exercise barriers for a client with Stage 2 Hypertension.

"Act as 'Mrs. Gable,' a 72-year-old client who is fearful of starting resistance training due to a previous back injury. I will act as your Exercise Specialist. Begin the session by telling me why you are hesitant to follow the PAGA guidelines I recommended."

Scenario 2: The "Hidden Trap" Audit

Objective: Spot the clinical contraindication during a routine intake interview.

"Act as 'John,' a 45-year-old with Type 2 Diabetes. You are eager to start a high-intensity program but you have a secret: you've been experiencing tingling in your feet lately (Peripheral Neuropathy). Don't mention it unless I ask the right clinical screening questions."

🏜️ Yuma Climate Adaptations

In the desert Southwest, heat index and hydration are clinical variables. Use these simulations to practice environmental safety audits.

Scenario 3: The Midday Warrior

Objective: Practice "Tactful Intervention" with a client who refuses to adjust their outdoor running schedule during a Yuma Heat Warning (110°F+).

"Act as 'Vince,' a 55-year-old dedicated runner who recently moved to Yuma. You insist on running at 2:00 PM because that's when you have free time. I am your Exercise Specialist. Use the PAGA environmental safety guidelines to convince me why I should (or shouldn't) worry about your schedule."

Scenario 4: The Dehydration "Hidden Trap"

Objective: Identify subtle signs of heat-related distress in a client with a history of Kidney Disease.

"Act as 'Elena,' a 60-year-old client with chronic kidney disease. We are 20 minutes into an outdoor Nordic Walking session in 102°F heat. You are starting to feel slightly dizzy and your speech is getting a bit slow, but you want to 'push through' to finish the loop. Do not admit you feel ill unless I specifically ask about your symptoms and hydration levels."
