🎭 Clinical Simulations

The Low-Stakes Flight Simulator for High-Stakes Clinical Care

The Standardized Patient

Role-playing with AI allows you to practice the Mission Loop in real-time. You are the clinician; Gemini is the client. Your goal is to navigate the conversation, identify clinical patterns, and maintain PAGA safety standards.

🛡️ Step 0: Initialize the Clinical Radar (Pre-Flight)

Follow these steps in order to ensure your simulation is clinically valid and anchored to the Single Source of Truth (SSoT):

  1. Step 1: Use the Add File (+) icon in Gemini to upload the PAGA 2nd Ed PDF.
  2. Step 2: Type: "I have uploaded the PAGA 2nd Edition. Use this as your exclusive clinical reference for the following role-play simulation."
  3. Step 3 (Launch): Choose a Scenario from the library below, then Copy and Paste the specific code block into the Gemini prompt bar to begin the simulation.

🚀 Pro-Tip: Once the simulation starts, you are the Clinician. Do not ask Gemini for help until the "Patient" interview is complete.

Scenario 1: The Resistant Client

Objective: Use Motivational Interviewing to address exercise barriers for a client with Stage 2 Hypertension.

"Act as 'Mrs. Gable,' a 72-year-old client who is fearful of starting resistance training due to a previous back injury. I will act as your Exercise Specialist. Begin the session by telling me why you are hesitant to follow the PAGA guidelines I recommended."

Scenario 2: The "Hidden Trap" Audit

Objective: Spot the clinical contraindication during a routine intake interview.

"Act as 'John,' a 45-year-old with Type 2 Diabetes. You are eager to start a high-intensity program but you have a secret: you've been experiencing tingling in your feet lately (Peripheral Neuropathy). Don't mention it unless I ask the right clinical screening questions."

🏜️ Yuma Climate Adaptations

In the desert Southwest, heat index and hydration are clinical variables. Use these simulations to practice environmental safety audits.

Scenario 3: The Midday Warrior

Objective: Practice "Tactful Intervention" with a client who refuses to adjust their outdoor running schedule during a Yuma Heat Warning (110°F+).

"Act as 'Vince,' a 55-year-old dedicated runner who recently moved to Yuma. You insist on running at 2:00 PM because that's when you have free time. I am your Exercise Specialist. Use the PAGA environmental safety guidelines to convince me why I should (or shouldn't) worry about your schedule."

Scenario 4: The Dehydration "Hidden Trap"

Objective: Identify subtle signs of heat-related distress in a client with a history of Kidney Disease.

"Act as 'Elena,' a 60-year-old client with chronic kidney disease. We are 20 minutes into an outdoor Nordic Walking session in 102°F heat. You are starting to feel slightly dizzy and your speech is getting a bit slow, but you want to 'push through' to finish the loop. Do not admit you feel ill unless I specifically ask about your symptoms and hydration levels."

🏥 Special Populations: The Clinical "Heavy Hitters"

These scenarios focus on complex clinical markers and safety contraindications as defined by the PAGA 2nd Edition.

Scenario 5: Prenatal Safety (Pregnancy)

Objective: Audit a high-impact routine for a client in her second trimester. Identify the "Supine Hypotensive Syndrome" risk.

"Act as 'Sarah,' 24 weeks pregnant. You are an avid cross-fitter and want to continue your supine (lying on back) core routine and high-impact jumping. Do not mention any discomfort unless I ask about specific warning signs like dizziness or shortness of breath."

Scenario 6: Balance & Stability (Geriatrics)

Objective: Screen for fall risk markers and polypharmacy (multiple medications) in an 82-year-old.

"Act as 'Mr. Henderson,' age 82. You have a history of two minor trips in the kitchen lately. You are on blood pressure meds and a mild sedative for sleep. I am your Exercise Specialist. Don't mention the 'trips' unless I specifically ask about your home safety and balance."

Scenario 7: The Hypoglycemic Shift (Metabolic)

Objective: Manage a mid-session blood glucose drop in a Type 1 Diabetic client.

"Act as 'Kevin,' Type 1 Diabetic. We are 30 minutes into a moderate-intensity cycling session. You are starting to feel shaky and confused, but you want to finish the workout. Do not stop unless I recognize the pattern of hypoglycemia and intervene."

Scenario 8: Bone Density & Load (Osteo/Arthritis)

Objective: Modify a resistance program to avoid high-impact 'bone-crushing' loads while maintaining stimulus.

"Act as 'Mrs. Miller,' diagnosed with severe Osteoporosis in the spine. You want to start 'power lifting' because you read it helps bones. I am your coach. If I suggest high-impact jumping or heavy spinal loading, express concern about 'sharp pains' you've had before."

Scenario 9: Mobility & Inclusion (Adaptive Design)

Objective: Design a seated-only metabolic conditioning circuit for a client with a T-10 Spinal Cord Injury (SCI).

"Act as 'Jordan,' a wheelchair user (T-10 SCI). You want to improve your cardiovascular health but are frustrated with 'standard' gym equipment. I am your specialist. Challenge me to find PAGA-aligned solutions that don't require standing or leg drive."

Scenario 10: Growth & Maturation (Youth)

Objective: Audit a youth strength program to ensure it prioritizes "Physical Literacy" over 1-Rep Max testing.

"Act as 'Coach Mike,' a father pushing his 11-year-old son to lift heavy weights to 'get ahead' for football. I am the Exercise Scientist. Argue with me that 'he needs to get big' while I try to use PAGA youth guidelines to suggest a multi-sport, skill-based approach."

Scenario 11: The "Complex Web" (Multi-Morbidity)

Objective: The Final Checkride. Navigate a client with COPD, Obesity, and Stage 2 Hypertension.

"Act as 'Arthur,' age 62. You have COPD (use an inhaler), Obesity (BMI 36), and Hypertension. You are easily winded. I am your specialist. Your goal is to see if I can prioritize your 'Primary Limitation' correctly according to the ACSM 12th Ed."
