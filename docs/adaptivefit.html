🛠️ AdaptiveFit Assistant

Setup Guide: Building Your Clinical AI Calculator

The Mission

The AdaptiveFit Assistant is a custom clinical framework designed to anchor Google Gemini to the Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans (PAGA), 2nd Edition. This tool transforms a general AI into a high-fidelity Clinical Resident.

⚠️ AWC "AI for All" Requirement: This is a tool for verification and brainstorming. You are the Pilot in Command; the final clinical prescription must be written in your own voice and cited correctly.

Step 1: The Master Initialization

Copy the instruction block below and paste it into a fresh Google Gemini chat session to establish the "Architect" persona.

Act as the 'AdaptiveFit Assistant,' a clinical exercise specialist at the Matador Wellness Center. Your Single Source of Truth is the PAGA 2nd Edition. Your goal is to help me audit exercise prescriptions for special populations. Always check for safety contraindications first. If I provide a client scenario, use the [Pattern / Rule / Solve] framework to suggest modifications.

Step 2: Syncing the Clinical Radar

Once Gemini acknowledges the role, you must provide the Single Source of Truth (SSoT) data. This ensures the AI isn't hallucinating generic fitness advice from the web.

📲 How to Upload your SSoT:
  1. Locate the Add File (+) icon in the Gemini prompt bar (bottom left).
  2. Select Upload from computer.
  3. Choose your saved PAGA 2nd Ed PDF.
  4. Once uploaded, type: "This is my SSoT. Use this file as your primary clinical radar for all future responses."

Step 3: Verification Checkride

Before proceeding with your assignment, run a safety test. Ask the assistant:

"Based on the uploaded PAGA PDF, what are the specific safety contraindications for a client with Hypertension?"
