🏗️ The High-Friction Philosophy

In clinical exercise science, the "fastest" answer is often the most dangerous. Our architecture uses High-Friction Learning to slow down the AI and force it to audit its own logic against the Single Source of Truth (SSoT).

🔄 The Mission Loop (Pattern / Rule / Solve)

To ensure student safety and professional accountability, every AI interaction must follow the Mission Loop. This is not just a format; it is a clinical safeguard that forces the AI to "show its work" before providing a prescription.

1. Pattern (The Clinical Intake)

The student identifies the specific variables of the client. "What is the age, the DXA T-score, and current medications?" Without a clear pattern, the AI cannot calculate risk.

2. Rule (The SSoT Citation)

The student forces the AI to cite the PAGA 2nd Ed or ACSM 12th Ed. "What is the specific rule for high-load exercise with this T-score?" This prevents the AI from using generic internet advice.

3. Solve (The Prescription)

Only after the Pattern and Rule are established does the AI provide the final "Solve." This ensures the exercise plan is built on evidence, not a hallucination.
