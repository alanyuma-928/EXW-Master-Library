# 🏛️ The Guided Learning Trifecta
**The Three Essential Roles of the Clinical AI Resident**

In the Matador Wellness Center Clinical AI Residency, we utilize a "High-Friction" pedagogical model designed to ensure that human judgment remains the primary driver of clinical care. Students must master three distinct roles when interacting with AI engines.

---

## 📐 1. The Architect
**Objective:** Engineering the Engine.

The Architect is responsible for the **P-T-C-F Engine Prompt**. They do not ask generic questions; they build precise clinical instructions that "lock" the AI into a specific professional persona and environmental context (e.g., Yuma's extreme heat).

*   **Key Skill:** Mastery of Persona, Task, Context, and Format.
*   **Mantra:** "The quality of the output is a reflection of the architecture of the input."

---

## 👨‍✈️ 2. The Pilot
**Objective:** Steering the Processing.

The Pilot provides the **Clinical Judgment**. They use the AI as a high-speed processor (the Engine) to run simulations or calculations, but they maintain total situational awareness. The Pilot decides when to "pull the lever" and when to "abort the mission" if the AI veers off-course.

*   **Key Skill:** Real-time evaluation and course correction.
*   **Mantra:** "Trust the engine for speed; trust the pilot for safety."

---

## 🕵️‍♂️ 3. The Auditor
**Objective:** Fact-Checking the Hallucination.

The Auditor is the **Guardian of the SSoT**. After the AI generates an output, the Auditor performs a mandatory **150-word Clinical Audit**. They cross-reference every clinical claim against the **Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans (PAGA)** or **ACSM** standards to hunt for hallucinations.

*   **Key Skill:** Rigorous evidence-based verification.
*   **Mantra:** "Measure Twice. Cut Once."

---
**Institutional Standard:** Aligned with AWC AI for All principles and the Einstein Defense protocol.
