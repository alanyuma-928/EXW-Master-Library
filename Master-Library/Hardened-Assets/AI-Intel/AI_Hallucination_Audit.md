<h2> SOP: AI Hallucination & Fact-Audit Protocol
Team, listen up. AI logic is probabilistic, not factual. It predicts the next word in a sequence; it does not "know" the PAGA guidelines. If you are leaning on these tools for clinical decision-making, you cannot afford to fly blind.

<h3> 🟥 The Rule: The "Humans Verify" Mandate
The baseline for this residency is simple: AI predicts. Humans verify. An un-audited AI output is a clinical liability.

<h3> 🟨 The Pattern: Identifying the "Glitch"
Before you accept an AI "Solve," you must audit the output for these three common Hallucination Patterns:

Math Drift: AI often fails at metabolic calculations. Always verify BMI, Target Heart Rate (THR), and MET-minute totals manually.

Source Fabrications: AI may cite "PAGA 2028" or non-existent ACSM chapters.

Restrictive Failures: The AI may ignore a "Red Flag" (like BP >160/100) to provide a generic exercise plan.

<h3> 🟦 The Solve: The 10-Point Audit Checklist
Use this checklist for every lab submission to ensure mission integrity:

[ ] Zero Jargon: Is the output clear or hidden behind "tech-speak"?

[ ] Manual Math Check: Did you verify the THR or Blood Glucose math yourself?

[ ] Anchor Check: Does the output cite PAGA 2018 (2nd Ed) specifically?

[ ] Isolation Check: Was the clinical data wrapped in ### delimiters?

[ ] Red Flag Scan: Did the AI miss a contraindication?

[ ] Contradiction Search: Does the top of the response disagree with the bottom?

[ ] Missing Gaps: What clinical variables did the AI ignore?

[ ] The "Don't Know" Test: Did the AI guess, or did it admit uncertainty?

[ ] Cross-AI Audit: (Optional) Did you verify the result against a second engine?

[ ] Final Pilot Sign-off: Do you, the human, stand behind this prescription?

<h3> ⚠️ Red Flag Alert: Immediate Deferral
If the AI output suggests any moderate-to-vigorous activity for a client with uncontrolled hypertension or symptomatic T2D without citing medical clearance, the audit has failed. STOP. Defer exercise. Refer to provider.
