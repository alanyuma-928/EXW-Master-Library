<h2> SOP: AI Study Strategies (Subject Mastery)
Team, listen up. Using AI to write your notes is a failure of leadership. Using AI to interrogate your understanding is how you become an expert. In this residency, we use AI to create a feedback loop that identifies your knowledge gaps before you ever walk into the MWC.

<h3> 🟥 The Rule: The "Don't Tell Me" Protocol
When studying a complex topic like Metabolic Equivalents (METs) or Hypertension Guidelines, never ask the AI to "explain it to you." Instead, ask the AI to test you.

The Goal: Force your brain into "Active Recall" mode. If you can't explain the concept to the AI, you don't understand it yet.

<h3> 🟨 The Pattern: Two Primary Mission Types
Choose your study mission based on your current knowledge level:

The Clinical Tutor (Deep Dive): "I am studying PAGA Chapter 6 (Older Adults). Act as my tutor. Ask me one question at a time about aerobic vs. muscle-strengthening requirements. Based on my answer, tell me if I am correct or if I missed a nuance, then ask the next question."

The Quiz Master (Assessment):
"Act as a Quiz Master. Create a 5-question multiple-choice quiz on the Absolute Contraindications for Exercise. Present all questions first, then grade my answers and provide rationale from the ACSM 12th Ed."

<h3> 🟦 The Solve: The "Study Driver" Template
Use this template to initialize a study session. Remember to wrap the SSoT text or assignment details in Structural Delimiters (###).

The Study Driver:

"I want to master [TOPIC]. Act as my personal tutor. Start by asking me a high-level question to gauge my current understanding. Use the SSoT provided below to verify my answers. Do not move to a more complex concept until I have demonstrated 100% accuracy on the current one."

<h3> 🏛️ The Architect's Verdict
AI is a mirror. If you use it to do the work, you stay weak. If you use it to challenge your brain, you become a "Centaur"—a human pilot with the speed of an engine and the judgment of a clinician.

"Study to prove yourself, not to complete the task."
