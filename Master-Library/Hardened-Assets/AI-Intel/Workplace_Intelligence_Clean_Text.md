## Workplace Intelligence: Mastering the "Clean-Text" Submission

In the modern **AI-integrated workplace**, formatting matters. For the rest of this course, you will use **Markdown (.md)** format to ensure your data is accessible, professional, and "AI-ready."

### Your Workflow: Google Docs to Canvas
Since we use **Text Entry** (copy/paste) for assignments, use this process to strip away "hidden code" buried in Word or PDF docs and keep your work professional:

1. **Draft:** Create your assignment in Google Docs. Use **Heading 2** for sections (Pattern, Rule, Solve). Use **Heading 3** for sub-sections.
2. **Export:** Click **File > Download > Markdown (.md)**. This saves a clean text file to your computer.
3. **Open & Copy:** Open that file with **Notepad** (Windows) or **TextEdit** (Mac), highlight the text, and **Copy** it.
4. **Paste:** Paste that text directly into the Canvas submission box.

---

### What Your Submission Should Look Like
When you paste your Markdown into Canvas, it should look exactly like this "Clean-Text" version. Note that all clinical case data and logic are wrapped in the required delimiters:

###
## Module Assignment: Physical Activity Audit
**Student:** [Your Name]

### Pattern
I identified a sedentary pattern among community college students during finals week, where activity drops by 40% due to increased study time.

### Rule
According to the **PAGA 2018 (2nd Ed.)**, adults should move more and sit less. Even small bursts of activity can reduce anxiety and improve cognitive focus during high-stress periods.

### Solve
I propose a "Walk-and-Talk" study session model. Students can review lecture notes via audio while walking the AWC track for 30 minutes, meeting the PAGA moderate-intensity aerobic guideline as they prepare for exams.
###

---

### Why We Do This
* 🤖 **AI-Ready:** This Markdown (.md) format is how AI tools and clinical databases process information without errors. See the [Structural Delimiters Protocol](./Structural_Delimiters_Centaur_Architecture.md) for technical requirements.

* ♿ **Accessible:** Using `##` (Heading 2) or `###` (Heading 3) makes your work readable by screen readers for those with visual impairments.
* 📱 **Mobile-First:** Your "Solve" can be read instantly on a smartphone, which is essential for community health workers in the field.
