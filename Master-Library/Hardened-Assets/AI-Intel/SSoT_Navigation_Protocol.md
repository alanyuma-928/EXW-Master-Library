<h2> SOP: SSoT Navigation Protocol (Digital Radar)
Team, listen up. In this residency, you are expected to find clinical answers in seconds, not minutes. We do not "read" the Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans (PAGA) like a textbook; we navigate it like a flight manual. The SSoT Navigation Protocol is your "Digital Radar" for finding the rules that save lives.

<h3> 🟥 The Rule: The "Search, Don't Sort" Philosophy
Information is only useful if it is accessible. We do not memorize page numbers; we utilize deep-search parameters to locate specific clinical constraints.

The Tools: Use Ctrl + F (Windows) or Cmd + F (Mac) to initialize your radar.

The Standard: Always keep the PAGA 2018 (2nd Ed) PDF open in a dedicated tab during every lab mission.

<h3> 🟨 The Pattern: Strategic Search Keywords
Don't search for broad terms like "exercise." Use "High-Fidelity Keywords" to jump directly to the safety thresholds you need:

Population-Specific: Search "Older Adults," "Pregnancy," or "Chronic Conditions."

Condition-Specific: Search "Hypertension," "Type 2 Diabetes," or "Osteoarthritis."

Safety-Specific: Search "Contraindications," "Medical Clearance," or "Safety."

<h3> 🟦 The Solve: The "Source Check" Verification
Once your radar finds a passage, you must perform a 3-step verification to ensure it applies to your current mission:

Step 1: Context Check. Are you in the correct chapter (e.g., Chapter 6 for Older Adults)?

Step 2: Threshold Check. Does the text specify an absolute limit (e.g., Blood Glucose < 100 mg/dL)?

Step 3: Citation Sync. Copy the specific phrasing and page number to include in your Presence Defense.

<h3> 🏛️ The Architect's Verdict
A Pilot who can't find their instruments is a liability. By mastering the Navigation Protocol, you ensure that every clinical "Solve" you generate is backed by the absolute authority of the federal guidelines.

"Speed is the byproduct of a sharp radar. Search with intention."
