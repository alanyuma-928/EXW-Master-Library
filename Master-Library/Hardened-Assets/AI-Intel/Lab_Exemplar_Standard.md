<h2> SOP: Lab Exemplar Standard (The Proof of Work)
Team, listen up. In this residency, an empty prompt is a zero. I do not grade your "intent"; I grade your execution. The Lab Exemplar Standard is the mandatory formatting template that proves you have successfully piloted the AI through a clinical mission.

<h3> 🟥 The Rule: No "Naked" Prompts
Submitting just the text of a prompt is like turning in a recipe instead of the meal. A complete submission must demonstrate the Centaur Protocol in action.

The Minimum Requirement: You must show the Prompt, the Raw AI Output, and your Presence Defense (Human Interpretation).

<h3> 🟨 The Pattern: The Anatomy of a Perfect Lab
Every high-scoring lab submission follows this specific visual and logical hierarchy:

Mission Header: Course ID, Module, and Mission Title.

The Clinical Driver (The Prompt): Must utilize the P-T-C-F Framework and be wrapped in ### delimiters.

The Raw Intel (The Transcript): The unedited response from Gemini, maintaining all tables and SSoT citations.

The Pilot's Audit (Presence Defense): 2–3 sentences of your own clinical judgment. Did the AI miss anything? Is the plan safe?

<h3> 🟦 The Solve: The "Exemplar" Layout
Use the following structure for every Canvas submission to ensure 100% compliance:

MISSION: EXW245 Lab 4.5 – Hypertension Screening

[1. THE PROMPT]

[P] Senior Clinician. [T] Audit BP for Client X. [C] Use PAGA Ch. 8.

[2. THE OUTPUT]
AI Table showing BP thresholds and required clinical actions...

[3. THE PILOT'S DEFENSE]
"The AI correctly identified the STOP threshold for BP >160/100 mmHg. However, I am adding a 5-minute seated rest period before the re-test to ensure accuracy, per ACSM 12th Ed guidelines."

<h3> 🏛️ The Architect's Verdict
The Exemplar Standard is how we maintain institutional agility. When every student submits in the same clean, searchable format, we can audit the health of the entire program in seconds.

"Show me the work. Defend the data. Protect the client."
