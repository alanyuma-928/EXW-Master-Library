<h2> SOP: AI Output Logistics (Top-Down Layout)
Team, listen up. In this residency, how you present your data is just as important as the data itself. We use a Top-Down Text-Only mandate. No screenshots. No formatting "bloat." Just clean, clinical intelligence.

<h3> 📜 The Standard Submission Architecture
Every lab and mission submission must follow this exact three-part sequence:

<h4> 1. Presence Defense (The "Why")
Before I look at the AI data, I need to see your brain at work. Write 2–3 sentences explaining your clinical intent.

Goal: Why did you choose this specific prompt? What safety threshold are you most concerned about (e.g., "I am auditing for hypoglycemia risk in a sedentary T2D client")?

<h4> 2. The Clinical Solve (The "Human-in-the-Loop")
This is your professional prescription. Based on the AI's data and the SSoT (PAGA/ACSM), what is the final verdict?

Goal: Translate the raw data into a safe, actionable exercise plan written in your own professional voice.

<h4> 3. The AI Transcript (The "Clean-Text" Audit)
Paste the raw conversation here. It must be formatted in Clean-Text Markdown.

Mandatory: The prompt must be wrapped in ### delimiters.

Mandatory: The output must maintain the original table structures or bulleted lists for readability.

<h3> ⚠️ Why "Text-Only" Matters
Searchability: I need to be able to use Ctrl+F to audit your SSoT citations. Screenshots are "dead data."

Accessibility: Screen readers cannot "read" a picture of a prompt. Text-only ensures we meet our WCAG 2.1 AA obligations.

Workforce Ready: In a clinical setting, you don't show a doctor a screenshot of a chat; you provide a clean, typed report.

<h3> 🛠️ Pro-Tip: The "Markdown Copy"
In Gemini, always look for the "Copy" or "Export to Docs" icon at the bottom of the response. This preserves the Markdown formatting (bolding, tables, headers) so you can paste it directly into Canvas without losing the Centaur Protocol structure.
