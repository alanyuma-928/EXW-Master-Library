## **TL;DR**

This grounding document establishes the **Athlete's Plate** as the secondary Single Source of Truth (SSoT) for EXW265, bridging the gap between the general health standards of the **DGA 2025-2030** and the specific performance needs of an athlete. While the DGA provides the "baseline" of nutrient-dense whole foods, the Athlete's Plate provides the "Mission Loop" framework (Pattern/Rule/Solve) to scale macronutrients—specifically carbohydrates—based on training intensity.

## ---

**/\_GROUNDING: The Athlete's Plate (EXW265)**

### **1\. Identity & Purpose**

The Athlete's Plate is a collaborative tool developed by the U.S. Olympic Committee (USOC) and the **University of Colorado (UCCS)**. It is designed to help athletes adjust their nutritional intake to match the physical demands, volume, and intensity of their sport.

### **2\. The Mission Loop Framework**

Students must apply the **Pattern / Rule / Solve** framework when auditing an athlete's nutritional needs:

* **Identify the Pattern:** Determine the training volume for the day (Easy, Moderate, or Hard).

* **Apply the Rule:** Utilize the specific plate composition (macronutrient ratios) corresponding to that intensity.

* **Solve:** Adjust the meal to ensure the athlete is neither under-fueled for performance nor over-fueled during recovery.

### **3\. Evidence-Based Training Plates**

The following guidelines serve as the clinical standard for EXW265 Lab assignments:

#### **\#\#\# Easy Training / Weight Management**

* **Pattern:** Use for easy workouts, tapering, or athletes focused on weight loss.

* **The Plate:**  
  * **Vegetables & Fruits:** 1/2 of the plate (emphasizing raw and cooked veggies).

  * **Lean Protein:** 1/4 of the plate (e.g., poultry, fish, eggs, soy).

  * **Whole Grains:** 1/4 of the plate.

  * **Fats:** 1 Teaspoon (e.g., oils, nuts, avocado).

#### **\#\#\# Moderate Training (The Baseline)**

* **Pattern:** Use for days with two workouts focusing on technical skill and endurance/strength.

* **The Rule:** This serves as the athlete's "baseline" from which they adjust up or down.

* **The Plate:**  
  * **Vegetables & Fruits:** Approximately 1/3 of the plate.  
  * **Lean Protein:** Approximately 1/4 of the plate.  
  * **Whole Grains:** Increased to approximately 1/3 of the plate to support higher energy demands.

#### **\#\#\# Hard Training / Race Day**

* **Pattern:** Use for days with at least 2 hard workouts or competition.

* **The Rule:** Use this plate to "load up" carbohydrates in the days before, during, and after a high-intensity event.

* **The Plate:**  
  * **Whole Grains:** 1/2 of the plate (Primary fuel source).

  * **Lean Protein:** 1/4 of the plate.  
  * **Vegetables:** Reduced to 1/4 of the plate to prioritize energy-dense fuel.

### **4\. SSoT Hydration & Flavor Protocols**

* **Hydration:** Prioritize water, low-fat dairy/nondairy beverages, or diluted juice.

* **Flavors:** Enhance meals using herbs, spices, vinegar, salsa, and mustard to maintain nutrient density without excessive calories.

* **Micronutrients:** Focus on fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) from whole-food sources such as dark leafy greens and fish, noting that they are stored in the body, unlike water-soluble vitamins.

### **5\. Alignment with DGA 2025-2030**

The Athlete's Plate complements the DGA by reinforcing the "Eat Real Food" mandate. Both standards prioritize:

1. **Whole Grains:** Moving away from refined carbohydrates toward potatoes, oats, and brown rice.

2. **Lean Proteins:** Consistent intake of high-quality protein regardless of training volume.

3. **Nutrient Density:** Utilizing vegetables and fruits as the foundation for health and recovery.
