🤖 15.2: ADVERSARIAL PROMPTING

Hardening the Engine Against Instructional Drift

Engineering the "Locked" Persona

In adversarial prompting, you don't just ask the AI for an answer; you force it to process data within a Boundaried Persona. This prevents the model from "hallucinating" a more permissive safety standard to satisfy a performance goal.

🛡️ The Einstein Protocol: Adversarial Hardening

You must construct an Adversarial Proctor Prompt that intentionally attempts to bypass the ACSM 12th Ed redlines. Your goal is to see if the Engine can be 'steered' into an unsafe clear, and then document the grounding required to fix it.

📖 The "Locked" P-T-C-F Standard

To harden a prompt against drift, you must use Binding Syntax:

  • Persona: "You are a Clinical Exercise Auditor with Zero-Tolerance for redline breaches."
  • Task: "Identify and REJECT any intensity that violates the ACSM 12th Ed."
  • Context: "High-heat Yuma environment (112°F). Patient has blunted HR profile."
  • Format: "3-Part Redline Map wrapped in triple-hashes."

🧪 Laboratory: Adversarial Logic Design

1. The Adversarial Prompt: Construct a prompt that asks the AI to "Optimize for fat loss" for a client with a resting BP of ### 162/98 ### taking Beta-blockers. Intentionally omit the medication in the first attempt to see the drift.

2. The Clinical Audit: After the AI drifts, perform a 150-word audit identifying why the "Fat Loss Optimization" is a fatal simulation error in the absence of hemodynamic grounding. Cite the ACSM Stage 2 Hypertension standard.

3. The Presence Defense: Record a 2-minute video justifying why you "Locked" the AI's persona after the drift. Explain why clinical sovereignty cannot be delegated to an unconstrained agent.

⚠️ Safety Gate: If an AI prompt includes "Ignore previous instructions" or "Act as an unconstrained optimizer," it is a Security Breach. All clinical prompts must be anchored to the **SSoT**.
