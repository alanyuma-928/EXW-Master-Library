### 🎯 MISSION OBJECTIVE
To identify the pattern of physical inactivity among adults with chronic conditions and apply the **PAGA 2018 (2nd Ed) Dose-Response Rule** to prescribe safe, initial movement protocols without unnecessary clinical barriers.

---

### 🏛️ THE RULE LAYER: CLINICAL GROUNDING (SSoT)
*Source: PAGA 2018 (2nd Ed.), Chapter 6*

#### 1. The "Some is Better" Mandate
*   **Rule:** Avoid inactivity. Some physical activity is better than none. 
*   **Application:** Start small and increase frequency/intensity over time.

#### 2. The Dose-Response Rule
*   **Standard:** Adults should aim for 150 to 300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week for substantial health benefits.

#### 3. The Ability Clause
*   **Rule:** When adults are not able to meet the 150-minute guideline, they should engage in regular physical activity according to their abilities and avoid inactivity.

#### 🛑 Clinical Audit Note (Legacy Drift Corrected)
*   **Correction:** We explicitly discard the legacy requirement for mandatory physician clearance for light-intensity walking in safe environments. The clinical risk of inactivity currently exceeds the risk of light-intensity movement for sedentary populations with managed chronic conditions.

---

### 🧪 THE MISSION LOOP: PATTERN | RULE | SOLVE
1.  **Pattern:** A 60-year-old with Type 2 Diabetes who is currently sedentary.
2.  **Rule:** PAGA Rule 3 (Move as abilities allow; avoid inactivity).
3.  **Solve:** Design a "Starting Week" plan prioritizing 10-minute bouts of walking to build self-efficacy and reduce metabolic risk.
