Phase 1: Identifying the Metabolic Diagnosis Pattern

The Prescription Gap

When working with special populations, exercise is not just a "workout"—it is a clinical intervention. The first Pattern we must audit is the medical diagnosis. How does a condition like Type 2 Diabetes alter the body's response to movement?

As a practitioner, your mission is to find the "Sweet Spot" of intensity. Too little movement results in no metabolic change; too much can lead to hypoglycemia or injury. This week, we examine how chronic conditions change the Rules of prescription.

Grounding in the Authority

Our safety audits are strictly anchored in the SSoT (PAGA 2018). We focus on Chapter 6, which provides federal guidelines for adults with chronic health conditions.

"Physical activity is important for people with chronic medical conditions... it can improve their quality of life and reduce the risk of developing new conditions." (PAGA 2018, Ch. 6).

Before you design a plan, you must audit the specific mobility and metabolic restrictions of your client. Accuracy in spotting these patterns is the difference between a safe Solve and a clinical risk.
