# The Drill: Module 1 - The Chronic Disease Audit
## SSoT: PAGA 2018 (2nd Ed.) / Module 1 Mission Brief

### Question 1: The Inactivity Gap
According to the PAGA 2018, what is the most critical "Safety Rule" for adults with chronic health conditions who are currently sedentary?

A. They must obtain a physician's clearance before performing any movement.

B. They should avoid inactivity; some physical activity is better than none.

C. They should only perform high-intensity intervals to save time.

**Correct: B**

---

### Question 2: The Dose-Response
What is the target weekly duration of moderate-intensity aerobic activity for adults with chronic conditions to achieve substantial health benefits?

A. 30 to 60 minutes

B. 150 to 300 minutes

C. Exactly 500 minutes

**Correct: B**

---

### Question 3: Clinical Barrier Check
Do the PAGA 2018 standards require a mandatory physician's clearance for a sedentary adult with Type 2 Diabetes to begin a "slow-paced walking" program?

A. Yes, all chronic conditions require clearance.

B. No, the risk of inactivity is generally higher than the risk of light-intensity activity.

C. Only if the client is over the age of 50.

**Correct: B**

---

### Question 4: Efficacy Building
Why is a "10-minute bout" of exercise considered a valid "Solve" in Module 1?

A. Because the PAGA states that some is better than none and movement should be according to abilities.

B. Because 10 minutes is the maximum a human can walk.

C. Because it is easier to grade.

**Correct: A**
