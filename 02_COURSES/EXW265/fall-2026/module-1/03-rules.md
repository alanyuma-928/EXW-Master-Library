    <section id="rule-briefing">
        <h3 style="color: #000080; border-left: 5px solid #000080; padding-left: 15px;">Operational Authority</h3>
        <p>
            Working with Special Populations requires a dual-audit of clinical and environmental risk. For Mr.
            Hernandez, you must apply the safety gates defined in <strong>PAGA 2018 Chapter 6</strong> and the
            <strong>Yuma Thermal Safety Gate</strong>.
        </p>
        <p>
            Individuals with chronic conditions often have impaired thermoregulation. In the Yuma environment, the
            temperature "Redline" is an absolute safety gate (105&deg;F) that overrides all clinical exercise goals to prevent
            thermal failure.
        </p>
        <p style="background-color: #fff9db; padding: 15px; border-left: 5px solid #f59f00;">
            <strong>Clinical Audit Update:</strong> Mandatory physician "clearance" for light-to-moderate activity is explicitly discarded. PAGA 2018 dictates that adults with chronic symptoms should "avoid inactivity" as their primary safety rule.
        </p>
    </section>
