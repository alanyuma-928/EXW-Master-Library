### 🏗️ The Case Study: Coach Alan (Age 45+)
1. **The Pattern:** A 45-year-old male rucker presenting with a DEXA T-score of -1.5 (Osteopenia) at the femoral neck and +1.2 at the lumbar spine.
2. **The Rule (SSoT Audit):** 
   - **T-Score > -1.0:** Normal sovereignty.
   - **T-Score -1.0 to -2.5:** Low bone mass (Osteopenia); mandate loading gates.
   - **T-Score < -2.5:** Osteoporosis; mandate high-impact redlines.
3. **The Solve:** Audit the discrepancy between the hip and spine. Why is the spine "Sovereign" while the hip is "Fragile"?
