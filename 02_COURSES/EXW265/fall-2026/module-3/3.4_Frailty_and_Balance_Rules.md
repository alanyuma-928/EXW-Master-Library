## 📡 The Balance Dialect
<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border-left: 10px solid #FFC72C;">
  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Balance Audit</strong><br>
  Postural stability is the primary metric of mechanical sovereignty and fall prevention. We audit neuromotor tasks and frailty levels to identify "Stability Redlines" and ensure the safety of movement interventions.
</div>

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Balance Pillars (ACSM Standards)
1. **The Stability Gate:** Prescribing balance-enhancing activities (e.g., heel-to-toe walking, one-leg stands) for at least 2-3 days per week.
2. **The Frailty Rule:** Auditing for clinical signs of frailty (e.g., slow gait speed, unintentional weight loss) to adjust intensity and complexity.
3. **The Environmental Pattern:** Auditing for tripping hazards and ensuring adequate lighting and support during balance interventions.

Output a Balance Table identifying any 'Stability Failures' or 'Environmental Smugglers' and suggesting a specific 'Balance Solve' (Format)."
</div>

&nbsp;

<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border: 2px solid #FFC72C; border-radius: 8px;">
  <h4>⚠️ The Unsupported Redline</h4>
  A logic collapse occurs when a clinician prescribes high-difficulty balance tasks to a frail patient without auditing for the availability of support (e.g., a chair or wall). In The WPE ecosystem, we audit for safety. If the solve ignores the support redline, the audit fails.
</div>
