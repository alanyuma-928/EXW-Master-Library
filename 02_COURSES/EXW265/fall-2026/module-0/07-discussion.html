Establishing the Network

Before we begin auditing clinical safety gates for high-risk populations, we must establish our professional network. In EXW 265, the Mission Loop Discussion serves as our orientation briefing. Use this space to introduce yourself and practice the technical protocols required for prescribing exercise as medicine.

Submission Protocol

To ensure high-fidelity communication from Day 1, your post must follow this specific two-phase rhythm:

Part 1: The Initial Briefing (Due Thursday)

Provide a brief introduction including three interests related to chronic conditions or inclusive fitness and one specific goal you want to achieve this semester. You must wrap your introduction in triple-hashes (###) to demonstrate protocol readiness.

Part 2: The Peer Audit (Due Sunday)

Respond to at least two of your colleagues. Find one common professional interest and welcome them to the mission. High-fidelity practitioners build networks; use this time to identify potential study partners for our PAGA Safety Audits.

10/10 Student Exemplar

Use the following as a model for your own post:

Initial Briefing (Thursday Post):

###
1. I am interested in adaptive exercise for individuals with Parkinson's Disease.
2. I volunteer with a local senior center leadings mobility drills.
3. I am a nursing major looking to specialize in geriatric care.
Goal: My goal for EXW 265 is to master the PAGA 2018 safety gates so I can confidently prescribe activity for individuals with multiple chronic conditions.
###

Peer Audit / Connection (Sunday Reply):

"Hello, Practitioner! I noticed you are interested in Parkinson's care. I am focusing on multiple sclerosis this semester, and I think our audits of neurological conditions will have a lot of crossover. Let's make sure we audit each other's Lab 05 results!"

SEAL Criteria for Orientation

  • Exemplary (10 pts): Zero protocol errors. Includes all three facts and a course goal wrapped correctly in ### delimiters. Professional engagement.
  • Competent (7 pts): Safe and functional. Meets requirements but may have minor formatting issues or missing delimiters.
  • Recalibrate (0 pts): Critical protocol failure. Failure to use delimiters or unprofessional conduct.
