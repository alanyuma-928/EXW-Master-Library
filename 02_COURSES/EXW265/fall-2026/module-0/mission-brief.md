### 🎯 MISSION OBJECTIVE
To establish the foundational safety protocols and clinical grounding required for prescribing exercise to special populations (Pregnancy, Chronic Conditions, and Disabilities) using the **PAGA 2018 (2nd Ed) Guidelines**.

---

### 🏛️ THE RULE LAYER: CLINICAL GROUNDING (SSoT)
*Source: ssot/paga-exw265.md*

#### 1. Pregnancy & Postpartum Protocol
*   **Aerobic Goal:** 150 minutes per week of moderate-intensity activity.
*   **Safety Gate:** Sustained vigorous activity is permitted only for those habitually engaged in it prior to pregnancy.
*   **Clinical Oversite:** Must be under the care of a healthcare provider.

#### 2. Chronic Conditions & Disabilities Protocol
*   **Aerobic Goal:** 150–300 minutes of moderate-intensity activity per week.
*   **Muscle Strengthening:** Minimum 2 days per week involving all major muscle groups.
*   **Safety Adaptation:** If unable to meet guidelines, stay as physically active as abilities allow; avoid inactivity.

#### 3. General Safety Rules (Chapter 6)
*   **Start Low, Go Slow:** Increase activity gradually to minimize adverse events.
*   **Regional Protection:** Use appropriate gear and choose safe environments (Reference: *Yuma Thermal Safety Gate*).
*   **Clinical Consultation:** Chronic symptoms require professional guidance, but individuals with chronic conditions should engage in regular physical activity according to their abilities and avoid inactivity (PAGA 2018). Mandatory physician "clearance" is not required for starting light-to-moderate activity unless specific Redlines are triggered.
