### 🏗️ Mission Task
1. **The Post:** Share your **Clinical Audit Table** (from Unit 2.2). Identify one "Medication Redline" you discovered (e.g., a blunted heart rate response due to Beta-Blockers or post-exercise hypotension due to ACE Inhibitors).
2. **The Audit (Reply to ONE peer):** Perform a "Clinical Sovereignty Audit" on a classmate's post. 
   * Did they correctly apply the **ACSM Medication Rule** (Unit 2.1)?
   * Is their **RPE-based Intensity Solve** (Unit 2.3) technically sound?
   * Identify one potential **Hemodynamic Redline** or **Pharmacological Logic Collapse** they may have overlooked.

### 🌟 10/10 Student Exemplar (Medication Peer Audit)
> "**Audit of Jane Smith's Clinical Solve:** I have evaluated your intensity interpretation. While your target heart rate calculation is correct for a healthy adult, your decision to use the Karvonen formula for a patient on Beta-Blockers is a **Pharmacological Logic Collapse**. Pattern: Resting HR at 52 bpm, Peak HR at 108 bpm. Rule: ACSM 12th Ed mandates RPE-based auditing (12-14) to bypass the suppressed chronotropic redline. Solve: You should have transitioned the audit to subjective intensity to maintain cardiovascular sovereignty."
