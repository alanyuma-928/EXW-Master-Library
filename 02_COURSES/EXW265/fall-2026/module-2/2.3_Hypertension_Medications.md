  Clinical exercise does not occur in a vacuum. We audit the interaction between antihypertensive medications and physiological responses to ensure patient safety and clinical sovereignty.

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Medication Pillars (ACSM Standards)
1. **The Beta-Blocker Gate:** Identifying medications that blunt heart rate (HR) and blood pressure (BP) responses to exercise, requiring the use of RPE for intensity monitoring.
2. **The Diuretic Rule:** Auditing for increased risk of dehydration and electrolyte imbalance (e.g., Hypokalemia) during exercise in high-heat environments.
3. **The PEH Pattern:** Understanding how ACE Inhibitors and Calcium Channel Blockers increase the risk of Post-Exercise Hypotension (PEH), necessitating a prolonged cool-down.
