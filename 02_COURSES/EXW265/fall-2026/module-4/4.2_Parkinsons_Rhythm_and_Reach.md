## 📡 The Parkinson's Dialect
<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border-left: 10px solid #FFC72C;">
  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Motor Audit</strong><br>
  Movement amplitude and rhythm are the primary metrics of functional sovereignty for individuals with Parkinson's Disease. We audit "Big" and "Loud" movement patterns (Rhythm-and-Reach) to identify rigidity redlines and ensure movement fluidity.
</div>

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Parkinson's Pillars (Clinical Standards)
1. **The Rhythm Gate:** Integrating auditory (metronome) or visual (floor lines) cues to bypass the basal ganglia and improve gait fluidity.
2. **The Reach Rule:** Mandating large-amplitude, multi-joint movements (e.g., boxing, broad reaching) to combat bradykinesia and rigidity.
3. **The Complexity Pattern:** Progressively increasing the cognitive and motor complexity of tasks to challenge the patient's executive function and coordination.

<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border: 2px solid #FFC72C; border-radius: 8px;">
  <h4>⚠️ The Freezing Redline</h4>
  A logic collapse occurs when a clinician fails to audit for "Freezing of Gait" (FOG) triggers or ignores the need for external cues during complex motor tasks. In The WPE ecosystem, we audit for fluidity. If the solve ignores the freezing redline, the audit fails.
