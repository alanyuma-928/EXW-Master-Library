## 📡 The MS Dialect
<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border-left: 10px solid #FFC72C;">
  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Fatigue Audit</strong><br>
  Thermoregulation and fatigue management are the primary metrics of neurological sovereignty for individuals with Multiple Sclerosis (MS). We audit intensity patterns and environmental factors to identify "Exacerbation Redlines" and ensure the safety of movement interventions.
</div>

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ MS Pillars (Clinical Standards)
1. **The Cooling Gate:** Mandating pre-cooling or during-session cooling strategies (e.g., cooling vests, fans) to prevent Uhtoff's Phenomenon (symptom flare-up due to heat).
2. **The Fatigue Rule:** Auditing for clinical fatigue (not just muscle tiredness) and ensuring adequate rest intervals between sets and sessions.
3. **The Functional Pattern:** Prioritizing multi-joint movements and flexibility (Unit 4.4 symmetry) to combat spasticity and maintain ADL sovereignty.

  <h4>⚠️ The Uhtoff Redline</h4>
  A logic collapse occurs when a clinician prescribes high-intensity intervals in a non-ventilated room for an MS patient. In The WPE ecosystem, we audit for heat. If the solve ignores the core temperature redline, the audit fails.
