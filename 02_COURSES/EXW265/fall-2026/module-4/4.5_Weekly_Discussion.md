### 🏗️ Mission Task
1. **The Post:** Share your **Motor Audit Table** (from Unit 4.2) or **Stability Audit Table** (from Unit 4.3). Identify one "Neuro-Sensory Redline" you discovered (e.g., a failure in rhythmic cueing or a lack of horizon-lock during a balance task).
2. **The Audit (Reply to ONE peer):** Perform a "Neuro-Sensory Sovereignty Audit" on a classmate's post. 
   * Did they correctly apply the **ACSM Parkinson's/Vestibular Rule** (Units 4.2/4.3)?
   * Is their **Neuro-Safety Solve** (Unit 4.1) technically sound?
   * Identify one potential **Heat-Sensitivity Redline** or **Nystagmus Logic Collapse** they may have overlooked.

### 🌟 10/10 Student Exemplar (Neuro-Sensory Peer Audit)
> "**Audit of Jane Smith's Neuro-Sensory Solve:** I have evaluated your motor interpretation. While your inclusion of rhythmic cues is correct, your 'Stability Solve' of prescribing rapid head movements for a patient with acute vestibular dysfunction without auditing for horizon-lock is a **Nystagmus Logic Collapse**. Pattern: Severe vertigo and oscillopsia. Rule: ACSM 12th Ed mandates visual fixation (Unit 4.3) during initial habituation tasks to maintain equilibrium sovereignty. Solve: You should have transitioned the audit to gaze stabilization before rapid head turns."
