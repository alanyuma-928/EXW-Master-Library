### 🏗️ Vestibular Pillars (Clinical Standards)
1. **The Horizon-Lock Gate:** Mandating visual fixation on a stable object (horizon-lock) during balance and movement tasks to provide external sensory reference.
2. **The Gaze Rule:** Auditing for gaze stabilization (VOR) exercises to improve visual-vestibular integration and reduce oscillopsia.
3. **The Habituation Pattern:** Progressively increasing the speed and complexity of head movements to habituate the vestibular system to provoking stimuli.

  <h4>⚠️ The Nystagmus Redline</h4>
  A logic collapse occurs when a clinician prescribes high-difficulty balance tasks to a vestibular patient without auditing for symptom provocation (e.g., nystagmus or severe vertigo). In The WPE ecosystem, we audit for symptom triggers. If the solve ignores the dizziness redline, the audit fails.
