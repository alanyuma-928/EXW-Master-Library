# Welcome to EXW 265: Exercise for Special Populations
## Fall 2026 | Arizona Western College

---

### 1. The Narrative: Exercise as Medicine
Welcome to the most clinically focused course in the WPE department. In EXW 265, we investigate how targeted human movement serves as a primary intervention for individuals with chronic conditions and unique physiological needs. Our mission is to move beyond "general fitness" and design evidence-based activity protocols that prioritize safety and longevity.

> **Coach's Note:** In this course, the "Safety Gate" is our most important tool. We do not prescribe until we have audited the risk.

---

### 2. The Authority (SSoT)
All clinical auditing and programming in this course are strictly governed by:
* **PAGA 2018 (2nd Ed):** The exclusive Single Source of Truth for special population guidelines.
* We utilize the **Mission Loop (Pattern / Rule / Solve)** to identify clinical risks and solve for safe, effective movement.
* *External clinical standards are secondary to the federal guidelines outlined in the PAGA.*

---

### 3. The 3-Sentence Presence Defense (Academic Integrity)
Because we are dealing with clinical safety, your "Presence" in the data is mandatory. All program designs require:
1. Identification of the **Clinical Pattern** (the specific chronic condition).
2. Citation of the **PAGA Rule** governing that condition.
3. A 3-sentence **Presence Defense** explaining how you have audited the "Safety Gate" to ensure client stability.
