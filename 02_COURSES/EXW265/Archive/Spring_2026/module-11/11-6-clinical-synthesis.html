PART 2: THE AUDIT (150-WORD CRITIQUE)

The AI output successfully identified the primary pathologies but demonstrated Clinical Blindness in the Interaction Layer. It suggested high-repetition leg presses for glycemic control in ### T2DM ###. However, per PAGA 2018 (2nd Ed) (Ch. 11), a patient with resting BP of ### 168/104 ### is an absolute redline for high-intensity or heavy-load lower-body resistance due to the risk of acute hypertensive spikes. Furthermore, the AI suggested "treadmill walking" for bone loading, failing to recognize that with ### peripheral neuropathy ### and ### osteoporosis ###, the fall risk and subsequent fracture risk is significantly elevated. My audit corrected this by pivoting to recumbent cycling for glycemic volume and seated resistance training to maintain skeletal load without the hemodynamic strain of standing or heavy lower-body pressing. The AI's "Safety Gate" failure was grounded in its inability to prioritize the most restrictive redline: hemodynamic stability.
