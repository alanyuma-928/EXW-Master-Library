9.2: Arthritis Management

Learning Objective 2.1: Joint-Sparing Protocols

Clinical Context: Arthritis

Arthritis management in exercise science requires a "Joint-Sparing" approach. According to Clinical Safety (Strict), the goal is to maintain range of motion (ROM) and muscular support without exacerbating systemic or local inflammation.

[Image of the human knee joint showing anatomy of articular cartilage and synovial fluid]
Table 9.2: Exercise Prescription for Arthritis (Clinical Safety (Strict) Standards)
Component Guideline Clinical Safety Gate
Aerobic 150 min/week moderate intensity. Prioritize non-weight bearing (Aquatics/Cycling).
Flexibility Daily ROM exercises. Move through pain-free range only.
