EXW 101: MOD 1 - HISTORY OF PE

The Evolution Audit

🎯 THE MISSION: Phase 1.1

Scenario: A local Yuma elementary school board is reviewing their Physical Education budget. They cite a 1920s gymnastics manual as evidence that "PE is just calisthenics" and propose cutting "Physical Literacy" programs to focus solely on high-repetition bodyweight drills.

⚠️ THE HIDDEN TRAP: The board claims that "the old ways were better" and that modern PE standards—like the **PAGA 2018** emphasis on aerobic variety and muscle-strengthening—are "distractions" from traditional discipline.

Your mission is to use the Socratic AI Tutor to audit this "Traditionalist Trap." You must identify why a 1920s model is clinically insufficient for current Yuma demographics and defend the **PAGA 2018** standards as the only safe, evidence-based path for 21st-century youth development.

🧪 Laboratory/Practical Application

  1. The P-T-C-F Prompt: Draft a prompt for Gemini to compare the "Fitness for War" models of the 1940s with the "Physical Literacy" models of the 2020s.
  2. The Clinical Audit: Audit the AI's claims against the historical timeline found in your module resources.
  3. The Presence Defense: Write a 150-word defense of why understanding the *history* of PE is essential for a modern exercise scientist.

🏁 Check for Understanding

How did the discovery of poor fitness levels in youth during the mid-20th century (e.g., the Kraus-Weber test) directly influence federal PE policy?
