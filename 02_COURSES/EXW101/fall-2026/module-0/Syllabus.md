## I. Course Overview (The SSoT)
**Single Source of Truth (SSoT):** This course is anchored to the **Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans (PAGA) 2018 (2nd Edition)**. We utilize these federal standards to examine the history, philosophy, and professional disciplines associated with human movement.

**Course Goal:** Demonstrate an understanding of the nature of the field, identify career opportunities, and apply foundational kinesiology principles toward professional fulfillment.

## II. The Mission Loop Framework
This course utilizes the **Pattern / Rule / Solve** architecture to frame the study of human movement:

* **Pattern (Identification):** Explaining the nature of the field and summarizing the differences between sub-disciplines and research areas (AWC Outcome 2.1).
* **Rule (Standardization):** Differentiating the basic theories, concepts, and historical perspectives that drive the profession (AWC Outcome 2.2).
* **Solve (Application):** Identifying career opportunities and applying this knowledge toward personal career development (AWC Outcome 2.3).

---

## III. Institutional Learning Outcomes
Upon satisfactory completion of this course, you will:
1. Explain the nature of Kinesiology and differentiate between its various disciplines and research areas.
2. Differentiate the historical perspectives and theories that drive current physical education practices.
3. Identify professional organizations and career pathways within the field.
4. Summarize the role of physical activity in public health as defined by the PAGA.
