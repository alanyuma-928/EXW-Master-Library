    <section id="cultural-rules">
        <h3 style="color: #003366; border-left: 5px solid #003366; padding-left: 15px;">The Social Filter</h3>
        <p>A community's movement patterns are dictated by the "Rules" of their environment and economy:</p>
        <ul style="margin-bottom: 25px;">
            <li><strong>The Economic Rule:</strong> Socioeconomic status (SES) is one of the strongest predictors of physical activity. Higher SES often correlates with more "Leisure-time" movement; lower SES often correlates with more "Occupational" but less health-driven movement.</li>
            <li><strong>The Neighborhood Mandate:</strong> Safety, lighting, and "Activity Deserts" act as geographical redlines. If a neighborhood lacks these "Rules of Support," physical activity will naturally decline.</li>
            <li><strong>The Cultural Mirror:</strong> Community norms, heritage, and language influence the perceived "Value" of certain types of movement (e.g., dancing vs. weightlifting).</li>
        </ul>
        
        <div style="background-color: #fefce8; padding: 20px; border-left: 5px solid #ca8a04;">
            <strong>Sociological Logic:</strong> You cannot solve for health without solving for <strong>Access</strong>. A park built in a neighborhood with 0% car ownership and no bus routes is a "Structural Failure."
        </div>
    </section>
