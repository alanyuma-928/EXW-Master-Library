    <section id="adaptive-rules">
        <h3 style="color: #003366; border-left: 5px solid #003366; padding-left: 15px;">The Inclusive Filter</h3>
        <p>When movement is constrained by a chronic condition, the "Rules" shift to maintain benefit without risk:</p>
        <ul style="margin-bottom: 25px;">
            <li><strong>The Continuity Mandate:</strong> If an individual cannot meet the 150-minute rule due to their condition, they should still perform regular activity according to their abilities—avoiding inactivity is the primary rule.</li>
            <li><strong>The "Relative Intensity" Rule:</strong> For those with sensory or mobility impairments, intensity should be measured by the **Talk Test** or **RPE Scale (0-10)** rather than absolute heart rate targets.</li>
            <li><strong>The Medical Consultation Gate:</strong> For people with chronic conditions already under medical care, movement plans should be designed in consultation with their healthcare providers.</li>
        </ul>
        
        <div style="background-color: #fefce8; padding: 20px; border-left: 5px solid #ca8a04;">
            <strong>Inclusive Logic:</strong> Your mission is not to "Fix" the condition, but to "Bridge" the gap between the condition and the Federal Guidelines.
        </div>
    </section>
