    <section style="padding: 25px;">
        <h3 style="color: #000080; border-bottom: 2px solid #000080; padding-bottom: 10px;">The Evolutionary Anchor</h3>
        <p>Human movement began as a biological necessity—survival. From hunter-gatherer societies to the industrial revolution, movement was integrated into the fabric of daily existence. In this briefing, we track the transition of movement from a "way of life" to a "prescribed clinical intervention."</p>
        
        <h3 style="color: #000080; margin-top: 40px;">The Great Decoupling</h3>
        <p>With the rise of automation and sedentary workplace environments, movement and labor were decoupled. This clinical shift necessitated the creation of <strong>Kinesiology</strong>—the scientific study of human movement—to combat the chronic disease epidemic caused by physical inactivity.</p>
    </section>

    <section style="background-color: #e6e9ed; padding: 25px; border-radius: 4px; margin: 20px; border-left: 10px solid #000080;">
        <h4 style="color: #000080; margin-top: 0;">🧭 Pattern Recognition</h4>
        <p>Notice the pattern: as environmental movement decreased, clinical expertise had to increase. We are now in the "Active Medicine" era where movement is audited and prescribed with surgical precision.</p>
    </section>
