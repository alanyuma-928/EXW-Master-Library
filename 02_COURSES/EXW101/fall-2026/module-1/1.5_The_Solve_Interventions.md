        <h3 style="color: #000080; border-bottom: 2px solid #000080; padding-bottom: 10px;">The Solution: Bridging the Gap</h3>
        <p>Now that we have identified the <strong>Pattern</strong> (The Inactivity Gap) and applied the <strong>Rule</strong> (The PAGA Mandate), we must provide the <strong>Solve</strong>. A solve is a prescriptive action that moves the client toward the SSoT requirement.</p>
        
        <h3 style="color: #000080; margin-top: 40px;">Effective Clinical Strategies</h3>
        <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
            <li><strong>The 10-Minute Bout:</strong> Breaking activity into manageable chunks to overcome the initial psychological barrier.</li>
            <li><strong>Intensity Calibration:</strong> Gradually transitioning the client from light-intensity movement (e.g., walking) to moderate-intensity as per PAGA standards.</li>
            <li><strong>Strengthening Integration:</strong> Implementing two days of major-muscle group resistance training.</li>
        </ul>
    </section>

        <p>The "Solve" is where your clinical sovereignty is demonstrated. It must be specific, actionable, and 100% synchronized with the PAGA rules cited in Step 1.4.</p>
