## ---

**The EXW245 AI Philosophy: The "Human-in-the-Loop" Auditor**

In this course, the "Single Source of Truth" (SSoT) is the **ACSM 12th Ed,** and the secondary Source of Truth is the **PAGA 2018 (2nd Ed)**. Your policy should treat AI as a junior researcher who is prone to "hallucinating" fad diets. The student's job is to be the **Architect** who audits the AI's suggestions against federal rules.

### **1\. Permitted Use: The AI Tool Kit**

Students are encouraged to use Generative AI (specifically Gemini) for:

* **Math Verification:** Double-checking Macronutrient distributions or Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) calculations.  
* **Persona Simulation:** Creating "Client Profiles" to practice dietary analysis.  
* **Structural Assistance:** Using Markdown to organize Lab reports for mobile-first accessibility.

### **2\. The Delimiter & Integrity Protocol**

To maintain academic integrity and "Curriculum-as-Code" standards, all AI-assisted work must follow these technical requirements:

* **The Triple-Hash Rule:** Any prompt fed to an AI, or any raw output generated by an AI, must be wrapped in \#\#\# delimiters in the submission.  
* **The Mission Loop Requirement:** Students cannot simply "turn in" AI text. They must apply the Mission Loop:  
  1. **Pattern:** What did the AI identify?  
  2. **Rule:** Which Dietary Guideline or PAGA does this align with (or violate)?  
  3. **Solve:** How does the student correct the AI to ensure clinical safety?

### **3\. Prohibited Use: "Fad-Botting"**

* **SSO-T Violation:** Using AI to generate recommendations based on "Keto," "Carnivore," or other commercial trends that contradict the **Dietary Guidelines for Americans** is strictly prohibited.  
* **Blind Passing:** Submitting AI-generated content without the "Auditor’s Note" (the Solve portion of the Mission Loop) will be treated as an incomplete clinical assessment.
