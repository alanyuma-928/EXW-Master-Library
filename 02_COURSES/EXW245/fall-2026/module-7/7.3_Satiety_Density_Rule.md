  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Satiety Audit</strong><br>
  Hunger management is the primary metric of adherence sovereignty. We audit "Satiety-Density Rules" and energy-to-volume ratios to identify "Density Failures" and ensure the long-term efficacy of weight management interventions.

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Satiety Pillars (ACSM Standards)
1. **The Volume Gate:** Mandating the intake of low-energy-density/high-volume foods (e.g., leafy greens, fibrous vegetables) to maximize gastric stretch and satiety sovereignty.
2. **The Fiber Rule:** Auditing for 25-35 grams of fiber per day to slow digestion and improve metabolic-protective sovereignty.
3. **The Hydration Pattern:** Prioritizing water-rich foods (e.g., soups, fruits) to increase meal volume without increasing caloric density or equilibrium logic collapse.

  A logic collapse occurs when a clinician prioritizes high-density liquid calories (e.g., juices, sodas) over whole-food volume for a weight-loss patient. In The WPE ecosystem, we audit for satiety. If the solve ignores the liquid-calorie redline, the audit fails.
