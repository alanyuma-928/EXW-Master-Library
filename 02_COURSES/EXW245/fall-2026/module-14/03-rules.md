    <section id="periodization-rules">
        <h3 style="color: #003366; border-left: 5px solid #003366; padding-left: 15px;">The Periodization Audit</h3>
        <ul style="margin-bottom: 25px;">
            <li><strong>The Macro-Cycle Rule:</strong> The largest block of time (typically 1 year or a full competitive season) represent the final mission objective.</li>
            <li><strong>The Meso-Cycle Gate:</strong> Intermediate blocks (2-4 months) must have a specific physiological focus (e.g., Hypertrophy phase followed by a Strength phase).</li>
            <li><strong>The Micro-Cycle Mandate:</strong> The smallest unit of training (typically 1 week) addresses the daily FITT-VP variables and specific recovery sessions.</li>
        </ul>
        
        <div style="background-color: #fefce8; padding: 20px; border-left: 5px solid #ca8a04;">
            <strong>The Principle of Reversibility:</strong> Fitness gains are temporary. If the Micro-cycle consistency is lost for more than 2-4 weeks, the Macro-cycle mission is compromised.
        </div>
    </section>
