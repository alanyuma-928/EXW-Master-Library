Orientation: Introductions & Clinical Assessment Goals

Establishing the Network

Before we begin auditing biometric data in GY 116, we must establish our professional network. In EXW 245, the Mission Loop Discussion serves as our orientation briefing. Use this space to introduce yourself and practice the technical protocols required for clinical exercise testing.

Submission Protocol

To ensure high-fidelity communication from Day 1, your post must follow this specific two-phase rhythm:

Part 1: The Initial Briefing (Due Thursday)

Provide a brief introduction including three interests related to clinical testing or prescription and one specific goal you want to achieve this semester. You must wrap your introduction in triple-hashes (###) to demonstrate protocol readiness.

Part 2: The Peer Audit (Due Sunday)

Respond to at least two of your colleagues. Find one common clinical interest and welcome them to the mission. High-fidelity practitioners build networks; use this time to identify potential lab partners for our Tuesday sessions.

10/10 Student Exemplar

Use the following as a model for your own post:

Initial Briefing (Thursday Post):

###
1. I am interested in cardiac rehabilitation and ECG interpretation.
2. I currently work as a physical therapy aide at a local clinic.
3. My goal is to eventually work in a clinical exercise physiology lab.
Goal: My goal for EXW 245 is to master the Lode Corival systems and accurately identify clinical safety gates.
###

Peer Audit / Connection (Sunday Reply):

"Hello, Practitioner! I also share an interest in clinical lab work. I'm looking forward to auditing VO2 max data with you in GY 116. Let's make sure we compare our AI math audits during Module 3!"

SEAL Criteria for Orientation

  • Exemplary (10 pts): Zero protocol errors. Includes all three facts and a course goal wrapped correctly in ### delimiters. Professional engagement.
  • Competent (7 pts): Safe and functional. Meets requirements but may have minor formatting issues or missing delimiters.
  • Recalibrate (0 pts): Critical protocol failure. Failure to use delimiters or unprofessional conduct.
