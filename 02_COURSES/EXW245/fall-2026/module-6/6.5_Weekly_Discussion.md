### 🏗️ Mission Task
1. **The Post:** Share your **Flexibility Audit Table** (from Unit 6.1) or **Neuromotor Audit Table** (from Unit 6.2). Identify one "Hypermobility Redline" you discovered (e.g., a failure in contraction duration or a lack of stability during a balance task).
2. **The Audit (Reply to ONE peer):** Perform a "Musculoskeletal Sovereignty Audit" on a classmate's post. 
   * Did they correctly apply the **PNF-Inhibition Rule** (Unit 6.1)?
   * Is their **Proprioceptive Solve** (Unit 6.2) technically sound?
   * Identify one potential **Syncope Redline** or **Inhibition Logic Collapse** they may have overlooked.

### 🌟 10/10 Student Exemplar (Hypermobility Peer Audit)
> "**Audit of Jane Smith's Flexibility Solve:** I have evaluated your PNF interpretation. While your inclusion of autogenic inhibition is correct, your 'Flexibility Solve' of prescribing PNF stretching for a patient with existing joint hypermobility (Beighton Score 7/9) without auditing for joint stability is an **Inhibition Logic Collapse**. Pattern: Excessive ROM without motor control. Rule: ACSM 12th Ed mandates balancing flexibility with neuromotor training (Unit 6.2) to maintain musculoskeletal sovereignty. Solve: You should have transitioned the audit to a stability-first neuromotor task."
