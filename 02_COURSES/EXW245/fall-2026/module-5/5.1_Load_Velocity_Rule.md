## 📡 The Resistance Dialect
<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border-left: 10px solid #FFC72C;">
  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Resistance Audit</strong><br>
  Force production is the primary metric of neuromuscular sovereignty. We audit the load-velocity relationship to identify "Velocity Failures" and ensure the safety and efficacy of strength and power interventions.
</div>

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Resistance Pillars (ACSM Standards)
1. **The Intensity Gate:** Mandating loads of 60-80% 1-RM for strength development and 30-60% 1-RM for power development (Unit 3.3 symmetry).
2. **The Velocity Rule:** Auditing for the "Explosive Concentric" phase in power-based prescriptions to maximize motor unit recruitment.
3. **The Volume Pattern:** Ensuring a minimum of 2-3 days per week with 2-4 sets per muscle group to maintain musculoskeletal sovereignty.

<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border: 2px solid #FFC72C; border-radius: 8px;">
  <h4>⚠️ The Grind Redline</h4>
  A logic collapse occurs when a clinician prescribes power training but fails to audit for movement velocity (e.g., the movement becomes a "grind" due to excessive load). In The WPE ecosystem, we audit for speed. If the solve ignores the velocity redline, the audit fails.
