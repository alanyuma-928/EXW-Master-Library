            03: The Rules - 1-RM Estimation & Safety
        </h2>
        <p style="font-weight: bold; color: #003366;">SSoT: ACSM 11/12th Edition Resistance Testing Standards</p>

    <section id="resistance-rules">
        <h3 style="color: #003366; border-left: 5px solid #003366; padding-left: 15px;">The Muscular Audit</h3>
        <ul style="margin-bottom: 25px;">
            <li><strong>The Estimation Rule:</strong> For safety in a classroom lab, we utilize the 1-RM Estimation formula (e.g., Baechle: 1-RM = weight * (1 + 0.033 * reps)) for any set exceeding 10 repetitions.</li>
            <li><strong>The anatomical Safety Gate:</strong> Maintain "Neutral Spine" and "Full Range of Motion" (ROM). Missions are redlined if the subject utilizes momentum (cheating) or exhibits <strong>Valsalva Maneuver</strong> (holding breath), which spikes blood pressure.</li>
            <li><strong>The Spotting Mandate:</strong> Any exercise involving a weight moving over the face or head (e.g., Bench Press) requires a mandatory spotter following the "Handshake Agreement."</li>
        </ul>
        
        <div style="background-color: #fef2f2; padding: 20px; border-left: 5px solid #dc2626;">
            <strong>Critical Restriction:</strong> 1-RM testing should be avoided in subjects with uncontrolled hypertension, advanced osteoporosis, or known cardiovascular disease (Refer to Module 2 Screening).
        </div>
    </section>
