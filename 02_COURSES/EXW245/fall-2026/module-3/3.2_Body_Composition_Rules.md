## 📡 The Anthropometric Dialect
<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border-left: 10px solid #FFC72C;">
  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Anthropometric Audit</strong><br>
  Body composition is a primary metric of health-related fitness. We audit anthropometric patterns to identify clinical redlines and ensure the accuracy of body fat percentage estimations.
</div>

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Measurement Pillars (ACSM Standards)
1. **The Skinfold Gate:** Mastering the 7-site and 3-site skinfold techniques (e.g., Chest, Triceps, Subscapular).
2. **The Procedural Rule:** All measurements must be taken on the *right* side of the body with the subject standing upright.
3. **The Accuracy Pattern:** Duplicate measurements must be taken at each site. If they differ by more than 2 mm, a third measurement must be taken to maintain instructional sovereignty.

  <h4>⚠️ The Precision Redline</h4>
  A logic collapse occurs when a clinician takes measurements on the left side of the body or fails to wait 1-2 seconds before reading the caliper. In The WPE ecosystem, we audit for precision. If the solve ignores the procedural redline, the audit fails.
