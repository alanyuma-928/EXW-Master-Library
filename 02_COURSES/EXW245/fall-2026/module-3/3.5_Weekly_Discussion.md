## 📡 The Borderline Peer Audit
<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border-left: 10px solid #FFC72C;">
  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Clinical Huddle</strong><br>
  Accurate measurement auditing is the foundation of fitness assessment. We audit each other's anthropometric, aerobic, and mechanical interpretations to ensure fleet-wide precision and patient sovereignty.
</div>

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Mission Task
1. **The Post:** Share your **Accuracy Table** (from Unit 3.2) or **Interpretation Table** (from Unit 3.3). Identify one "Borderline Audit" you discovered (e.g., a measurement exactly 2 mm apart or a heart rate at exactly the steady-state limit).
2. **The Audit (Reply to ONE peer):** Perform an "Assessment Sovereignty Audit" on a classmate's post. 
   * Did they correctly apply the **ACSM 2 mm Variance Rule** (Unit 3.2)?
   * Is their **Steady-State Interpretation** (Unit 3.3) or **1-RM Progression** (Unit 3.4) technically sound?
   * Identify one potential **Precision Redline** or **Mechanical Logic Collapse** they may have overlooked.

&nbsp;

<div style="background-color: #FFFDD0; color: #000080; padding: 15px; border: 2px solid #000080; border-radius: 8px;">
  <h4>🛠️ The Power of One</h4>
  Focus on high-fidelity feedback. A single, rigorous audit of a peer's assessment logic is worth more than a dozen generic "good job" comments. Use specific ACSM procedural citations in your feedback.
</div>

&nbsp;

### 🌟 10/10 Student Exemplar (Borderline Peer Audit)
> "**Audit of Jane Smith's Aerobic Solve:** I have evaluated your aerobic interpretation. While your VO2max calculation is correct, your decision to progress the YMCA stage when the heart rate only stabilized for 1 minute is an **Accuracy Logic Collapse**. Pattern: HR at 140 bpm, then 142 bpm. Rule: ACSM 12th Ed mandates *two* consecutive minutes of steady state. Solve: You should have extended the stage by 1 minute to maintain metabolic sovereignty."
