## 📡 The Testing Peer Audit
<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border-left: 10px solid #FFC72C;">
  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Clinical Huddle</strong><br>
  Accurate safety auditing is the foundation of exercise testing. We audit each other's contraindication interpretations and emergency protocols to ensure fleet-wide testing sovereignty.

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Mission Task
1. **The Post:** Share your **Safety Table** (from Unit 1.3). Identify one "Absolute Redline" you discovered and your proposed "Safety Solve".
2. **The Audit (Reply to ONE peer):** Perform a "Testing Sovereignty Audit" on a classmate's post. 
   * Did they correctly distinguish between **Absolute and Relative** contraindications?
   * Is their **Safety Solve** (Unit 1.3) safe for the identified population profile (Unit 1.1)?
   * Identify one potential **Hemodynamic Redline** or **Termination Logic Collapse** they may have overlooked.

&nbsp;

<div style="background-color: #FFFDD0; color: #000080; padding: 15px; border: 2px solid #000080; border-radius: 8px;">
  <h4>🛠️ The Power of One</h4>
  Focus on high-fidelity feedback. A single, rigorous audit of a peer's testing logic is worth more than a dozen generic "good job" comments. Use specific ACSM rule citations in your feedback.
</div>

&nbsp;

### 🌟 10/10 Student Exemplar (Testing Peer Audit)
> "**Audit of Jane Smith's Safety Solve:** I have evaluated your safety table. While your identification of Acute MI as an absolute contraindication is correct, your 'Safety Solve' of continuing with a submaximal test is a **Safety Logic Collapse**. Pattern: Acute MI is an *absolute* redline. Rule: ACSM 12th Ed mandates NO testing until the condition is stabilized. Solve: The test must be canceled immediately to maintain clinical sovereignty."
