Phase 1: The Clinical Symptom Pattern

Mission Briefing

In GY 116, safety is our first priority. Before "Mr. Jackson" can participate in his assessment, you must audit his health history. Mr. Jackson is a 55-year-old male wishing to start a moderate-intensity weight-lifting program. Identifying clinical risks before testing is a non-negotiable professional standard.

Your goal is to extract the raw clinical data below and prepare it for a risk stratification audit against our dual authorities: the Abridged ACSM 12th Ed SSoT and the PAGA 2018.

AI Socratic Audit: Prompt Template

Dual-Authority Protocol: You must click Add File (+) in Gemini to attach BOTH the Abridged ACSM 12th Ed SSoT (Markdown) and the PAGA 2018 PDF before sending the prompt below.

Copy the following block exactly for your interaction in SOP 04:

Socratic Tutor, audit this screening history using the Add File (+) Abridged ACSM 12th Ed SSoT and the PAGA 2018 PDF:

###
Client: Mr. Jackson (55yo)
Current Activity: Sedentary (0 mins/week for last 6 months)
Medical History:
- Known Disease: No diagnosis of CVD, Metabolic, or Renal disease.
- Signs/Symptoms: Reports occasional "tightness" in chest during brisk walks to the mailbox; reports "dizziness" when standing up quickly.
- Goal: Start moderate-intensity exercise.
###
