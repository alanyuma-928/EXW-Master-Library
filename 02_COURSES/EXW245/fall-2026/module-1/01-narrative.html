The Screening Narrative

Phase 1: Identifying Clinical Signs & Symptoms

The Safety Gap

In clinical exercise science, the first Pattern we audit is not how fast a client can run, but whether it is safe for them to run at all. Every year, cardiovascular events during exercise occur because practitioners bypassed the "Safety Gates" of pre-participation screening.

This week, we begin our hybrid journey by mastering the art of the clinical interview. Your goal is to identify the signs and symptoms of cardiovascular, metabolic, or renal disease. Identifying these patterns before the client touches a piece of equipment in GY 116 is your primary professional responsibility.

Grounding in the Authority

Our screening logic is anchored in the SSoT (ACSM 12th Ed). We utilize the ACSM screening algorithm to determine if a client needs medical clearance before starting a moderate or vigorous exercise program.

"The ACSM pre-participation screening algorithm is designed to identify individuals at risk for exercise-related sudden cardiac death or acute myocardial infarction." (ACSM 12th Ed, Ch. 2).

As you review the Case Study, look for "Red Flags": dyspnea, syncope, or edema. These are the Rules that dictate your next move in the clinical solve.
