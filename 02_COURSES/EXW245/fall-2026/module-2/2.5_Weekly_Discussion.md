## 📡 The Liability Peer Audit

  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Clinical Huddle</strong><br>
  Accurate legal auditing is the foundation of clinical practice. We audit each other's screening and consent interpretations to ensure fleet-wide liability protection and patient sovereignty.

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Mission Task
1. **The Post:** Share your **Screening Table** (from Unit 2.1). Identify one "Liability Redline" you discovered (e.g., missing signature or ignored symptom).
2. **The Audit (Reply to ONE peer):** Perform a "Liability Sovereignty Audit" on a classmate's post. 
   * Did they correctly apply the **ACSM Screening Algorithm** (Unit 2.1)?
   * Is their **Informed Consent Solve** (Unit 2.3) legally sound?
   * Identify one potential **Autonomy Redline** or **Symptom Logic Collapse** they may have overlooked.

&nbsp;

<div style="background-color: #FFFDD0; color: #000080; padding: 15px; border: 2px solid #000080; border-radius: 8px;">
  <h4>🛠️ The Power of One</h4>
  Focus on high-fidelity feedback. A single, rigorous audit of a peer's legal logic is worth more than a dozen generic "good job" comments. Use specific ACSM rule citations in your feedback.
</div>

&nbsp;

### 🌟 10/10 Student Exemplar (Liability Peer Audit)
> "**Audit of Jane Smith's Screening Solve:** I have evaluated your screening table. While your identification of CMR disease is correct, your 'Consent Solve' of having the participant sign *after* the initial submaximal test is a **Legal Logic Collapse**. Pattern: Data collection began before consent. Rule: ACSM 12th Ed mandates signature *before* any testing. Solve: The test results are legally void; the protocol must be restarted to maintain clinical sovereignty."
