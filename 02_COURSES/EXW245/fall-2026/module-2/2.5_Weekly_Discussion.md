## 📡 The Intake Peer Audit
<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border-left: 10px solid #FFC72C;">
  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Clinical Huddle</strong><br>
  Accurate intake is the difference between a safe prescription and a clinical failure. We audit each other's screening logic and metric interpretations to ensure fleet-wide safety.
</div>

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Mission Task
1. **The Post:** Share your **Intake Summary Table** (from Units 2.2 and 2.3). Identify the most critical clinical redline you discovered during your subjective or objective audit.
2. **The Audit (Reply to ONE peer):** Perform a "Screening Sovereignty Audit" on a classmate's post. 
   * Did they correctly apply the **ACSM 12th Ed Algorithm** (Unit 2.1)?
   * Is their interpretation of resting vitals (BP/HR) accurate?
   * Identify one potential "Consent Redline" (Unit 2.4) they may have overlooked in their intake process.

&nbsp;

<div style="background-color: #FFFDD0; color: #000080; padding: 15px; border: 2px solid #000080; border-radius: 8px;">
  <h4>🛠️ The Power of One</h4>
  Focus on high-fidelity feedback. A single, rigorous audit of a peer's screening logic is worth more than a dozen generic "good job" comments. Use specific ACSM rule citations in your feedback.
