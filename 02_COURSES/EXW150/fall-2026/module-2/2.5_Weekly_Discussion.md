## 📡 The Digital Label Audit
<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border-left: 10px solid #FFC72C;">
  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Clinical Huddle</strong><br>
  Accurate label auditing is the foundation of nutritional sovereignty. We audit each other's interpretations of the Nutrition Facts panel to ensure fleet-wide compliance with DGA 10% redlines.

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Mission Task
1. **The Post:** Share your **Redline Table** (from Unit 2.2). Identify one "Digital Label Redline" you discovered (e.g., a product where Added Sugar exceeds 10% of total calories).
2. **The Audit (Reply to ONE peer):** Perform a "Nutritional Sovereignty Audit" on a classmate's post. 
   * Did they correctly apply the **DGA 10% Added Sugar/Saturated Fat Rule** (Unit 2.2)?
   * Is their **Substitution Solve** (Unit 2.2) nutrient-dense and compliant with the 85/15 rule?
   * Identify one potential **Vitals Redline** (Sodium/Hydration) they may have overlooked in their audit.

&nbsp;

<div style="background-color: #FFFDD0; color: #000080; padding: 15px; border: 2px solid #000080; border-radius: 8px;">
  <h4>🛠️ The Power of One</h4>
  Focus on high-fidelity feedback. A single, rigorous audit of a peer's label logic is worth more than a dozen generic "good job" comments. Use specific DGA rule citations in your feedback.
</div>

### 🌟 10/10 Student Exemplar (Label Peer Audit)
> "**Audit of Jane Smith's Label Solve:** I have evaluated your redline table. While your identification of the high sodium content is correct, your 'Substitution Solve' of a 'Sugar-Free' beverage that contains artificial dyes is a **Density Logic Collapse**. Pattern: High-processing vs nutrient density. Rule: DGA 2025–2030 mandates 85% nutrient-dense sources. Solve: Swap for plain mineral water with a splash of 100% fruit juice to maintain nutritional sovereignty."
