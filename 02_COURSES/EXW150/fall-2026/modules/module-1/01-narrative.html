The Metabolic Narrative

Phase 1: Identifying the Energy Demand Pattern

The Fueling Gap

In sports nutrition, we often focus on the *what*—the calories and the macros. But as a practitioner, you must first understand the *how*. How does the body actually convert chemical energy into mechanical work?

The Pattern we are auditing this week is Bioenergetics: the flow of energy in a biological system. Every athletic movement, from a 100m sprint to a marathon, relies on a specific ratio of energy systems (Phosphagen, Glycolytic, and Oxidative). If the fuel in the tank (nutrition) doesn't match the engine's demand (intensity), performance fails.

Grounding in the Authority

Our nutritional solve must align with the SSoT (DGA 2025-2030). While energy systems are physiological, the substrate we use to fuel them (CHO, FAT, PRO) is governed by federal dietary standards for health and performance.

"A healthy eating pattern includes foods from all groups... in nutrient-dense forms to meet energy needs without exceeding caloric limits." (DGA 2025-2030).

Before you prescribe a meal plan, you must audit the metabolic demand. Are you fueling a high-intensity glycolytic "Pattern," or a steady-state oxidative one? Your math starts here.
