### 🏗️ Research Task
1. **The Search:** Use the AWC Library database to find one peer-reviewed article from the last 24 months related to **Micronutrient Bioavailability**, **Environmental Hydration (WPE)**, or **Phytonutrient Synergy**.
2. **The Audit:** Perform a "Micronutrient Intel Audit" using the following scaffold:
   * **Source:** (Full APA Citation)
   * **Pattern:** What is the specific metabolic trend or performance outcome identified?
   * **Rule:** How does this align with the ACSM and DGA standards (Units 3.1-3.3)?
   * **Solve:** How does this research improve the accuracy of your micronutrient audits?

&nbsp;

<div style="background-color: #FFFDD0; color: #000080; padding: 15px; border: 2px solid #000080; border-radius: 8px;">
  <h4>🛠️ The Metabolic Linter</h4>
  Prioritize research that evaluates the efficacy of mineral pairing (e.g., Iron + Vitamin C) or fluid repletion rates. If the study suggests a new clinical redline for absorption or hydration, flag its relevance to your Micronutrient Audit (Unit 3.1 or 3.2).
</div>

### 🌟 10/10 Student Exemplar (Micronutrient Research Audit)
> "**Source:** Williams, T. et al. (2024). Efficacy of Vitamin C Pairing on Non-Heme Iron Absorption. *MSSE*.  
> **Pattern:** Adding 50mg of Vitamin C to a plant-based iron source resulted in a 3x increase in serum ferritin levels compared to iron alone.  
> **Rule:** DGA mandates strategic pairing (Unit 3.1) to maintain metabolic sovereignty.  
> **Solve:** To maintain micronutrient sovereignty, all iron audits must prioritize Vitamin C pairing to bypass the absorption-failure redline."
