## 📡 The Hydration Dialect
<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border-left: 10px solid #FFC72C;">
  <strong>Sovereignty System: The Fluid Audit</strong><br>
  Euhydration is the primary metric of thermoregulatory sovereignty. We audit fluid intake and sweat loss patterns (The WPE Hydration Rule) to identify "Dehydration Redlines" and ensure the safety of metabolic interventions in high-heat environments.
</div>

&nbsp;

### 🏗️ Hydration Pillars (Clinical Standards)
1. **The Pre-Hydration Gate:** Mandating the intake of 5-10 mL/kg of body weight in the 2-4 hours prior to exercise to ensure baseline metabolic sovereignty.
2. **The Sweat-Rate Rule:** Auditing for body weight changes during exercise to ensure fluid loss does not exceed 2% of total body mass.
3. **The Electrolyte Pattern:** Mandating sodium and potassium replacement in environments where sweat loss is high (WPE >82°F) to prevent hyponatremia logic collapses.

<div style="background-color: #000080; color: #FFFDD0; padding: 15px; border: 2px solid #FFC72C; border-radius: 8px;">
  <h4>⚠️ The 2% Redline</h4>
  A logic collapse occurs when an athlete loses >2% of body mass during exercise without a corresponding increase in fluid volume or electrolyte density. In The WPE ecosystem, we audit for mass loss. If the solve ignores the 2% redline, the audit fails.
</div>
