    <section id="professional-rules">
        <h3 style="color: #003366; border-left: 5px solid #003366; padding-left: 15px;">The Professional Filter</h3>
        <p>Your nutrition career is governed by these "Rules":</p>
        <ul style="margin-bottom: 25px;">
            <li><strong>The Scope of Practice Rule:</strong> A "Nutrition Coach" can provide guidance, education, and behavioral support for healthy populations. They **CANNOT** prescribe therapeutic diets or provide Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT) for clinical conditions.</li>
            <li><strong>The RD vs. Coach Gate:</strong> In most states, only a Registered Dietitian (RD) has the legal authority to treat clinical diseases (e.g., Diabetes, Renal failure) through nutritional intervention. Cross this gate without a license, and you face legal mission-failure.</li>
            <li><strong>The NCCA Certification Mandate:</strong> To work as a professional "Sports Nutritionist" (without the RD), you must hold a credential accredited by the NCCA (e.g., CISSN or specialized ACSM certs).</li>
        </ul>
        
        <div style="background-color: #fefce8; padding: 20px; border-left: 5px solid #ca8a04;">
            <strong>Ethics Logic:</strong> Passion is not a **License**. Protecting your professional liability means knowing when to say, "That is outside my scope of practice."
        </div>
    </section>
