# EXW 150: Nutrition for Exercise and Sports
## Fall 2026 | Arizona Western College



---

## I. Course Overview (The SSoT)
**Single Source of Truth (SSoT):** This course is anchored to the **Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans (PAGA) 2018** and the **U.S. Dietary Guidelines for Americans (DGA) 2025-2030**. We prioritize a "Food First" philosophy, emphasizing whole-food nutrition over commercial supplements for athletic performance.

**Course Goal:** Develop a comprehensive understanding of the relationship between nutrition, fluid intake, and peak physical performance.

---

## II. The Mission Loop Framework
We utilize the **Pattern / Rule / Solve** architecture to master sports nutrition:

* **Pattern (Identification):** Recognizing physiological demands and nutritional requirements necessary for varying levels of physical activity (AWC Outcome 2.1, 2.3).
* **Rule (Standardization):** Applying federal guidelines to timing, fluid intake, and the pros/cons of supplementation (AWC Outcome 2.6, 2.7).
* **Solve (Application):** Analyzing the relationship between diet and training to create optimized performance plans (AWC Outcome 2.4, 2.5).

---

## III. Institutional Learning Outcomes
Upon satisfactory completion of this course, you will be able to:
1. Recognize physiological demands and analyze fluid intake for varying activity levels.
2. List nutritional requirements necessary for sports-related activities.
3. Describe the relationship between diet and training for optimum performance.
4. Discuss current theories related to weight management and peak performance.
5. Evaluate the timing of nutrient and fluid intake for optimal recovery and output.
