### 🏗️ Mission Task
1. **The Post:** Share your **Density Table** (from Unit 1.3). Identify one "Density Redline" you discovered and your proposed "Nutrient Solve".
2. **The Audit (Reply to ONE peer):** Perform a "Nutritional Sovereignty Audit" on a classmate's post. 
   * Did they correctly apply the **85/15 Nutrient Density Rule** (Unit 1.3)?
   * Is their **Plate Solve** (Unit 1.4) visually and clinically sound?
   * Identify one potential **Discretionary Redline** or **Proportion Logic Collapse** they may have overlooked.

&nbsp;

<div style="background-color: #FFFDD0; color: #000080; padding: 15px; border: 2px solid #000080; border-radius: 8px;">
  <h4>🛠️ The Power of One</h4>
  Focus on high-fidelity feedback. A single, rigorous audit of a peer's nutritional logic is worth more than a dozen generic "good job" comments. Use specific DGA rule citations in your feedback.
</div>

### 🌟 10/10 Student Exemplar (Nutritional Peer Audit)
> "**Audit of Jane Smith's Density Solve:** I have evaluated your density table. While your identification of fruits and vegetables is correct, your 'Nutrient Solve' of using a high-sugar yogurt as a protein source is a **Density Logic Collapse**. Pattern: Added sugar excess. Rule: DGA 2025–2030 mandates <10% added sugar. Solve: Swap for plain Greek yogurt with fresh berries to maintain nutritional sovereignty."
