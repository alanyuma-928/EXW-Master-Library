Module 18.0: Privacy & Nutritional Ethics

"The Ethical Redlines of Clinical AI."

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Analyze the ethical implications of data privacy in AI-assisted nutritional assessments.
  • Identify best practices for maintaining client confidentiality using the Delimiter Protocol.
  • Evaluate the risk of Personally Identifiable Information (PII) leakage in large language models.
  • Formulate a "Privacy-First" workflow for clinical data processing.

📖 Instructional Content

As we conclude our technical performance residency, we return to the most critical foundation: **Ethics**. In an era of Clinical AI, the practitioner acts as the final guardian of client data. This module explores the "Digital Redlines" that must never be crossed. You will learn to use the **Delimiter Protocol (###)** to quarantine sensitive variables and understand why de-identification is a non-negotiable step before any data enters an AI engine. We will align our practices with both the **Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics Code of Ethics** and modern data privacy standards (HIPAA/FERPA).
