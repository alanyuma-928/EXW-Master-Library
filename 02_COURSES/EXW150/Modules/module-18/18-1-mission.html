EXW 150: MOD 18 - ETHICS AUDIT

The Privacy Firewall

🎯 The Mission

A new client with multiple complex food allergies and a sensitive medical history requires a specialized meal plan. You intend to use an AI Engine to help calculate micronutrient distributions.

⚠️ THE HIDDEN TRAP: The "Convenience Trap"—the temptation to copy-paste raw clinical notes directly into an LLM to save time, effectively leaking sensitive patient data into a public model and violating the foundational ethical redlines of the residency.

Your mission is to use the Socratic AI Tutor to design a "Privacy-First" P-T-C-F prompt. You must demonstrate how to de-identify the client's data using the **Delimiter Protocol (###)**, ensuring that no personally identifiable information (PII) is exposed to the engine while still maintaining clinical precision.

🧪 Laboratory/Practical Application

  1. The P-T-C-F Prompt: Draft a prompt that wraps clinical case data in triple-hashes and uses generic identifiers (e.g., "Resident A") to protect privacy.
  2. The Clinical Audit: Audit the AI's generated response for any "implicit PII" (e.g., specific location references that were not requested).
  3. The Presence Defense: Write a 150-word defense explaining why the practitioner, not the AI, bears the ultimate responsibility for data integrity and ethical compliance.
