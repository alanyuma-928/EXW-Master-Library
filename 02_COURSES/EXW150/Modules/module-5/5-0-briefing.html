Module 5.0: Micronutrients & Performance

"The Metabolic Spark Plugs."

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Identify key vitamins and minerals required for aerobic and anaerobic metabolism.
  • Analyze the physiological consequences of sub-clinical micronutrient deficiencies (e.g., Iron, B12, Vitamin D).
  • Apply DGA 2025-2030 standards for meeting nutrient needs via "Food First" strategies.
  • Evaluate the safety and efficacy of common performance supplements against whole-food alternatives.

📖 Instructional Content

If macronutrients are the logs on the fire, **micronutrients** are the oxygen that allows them to burn. This module explores the intricate world of vitamins and minerals, focusing on their role as co-factors in metabolic pathways. Grounded in the **DGA 2025-2030**, we will emphasize nutrient density and the "Food First" protocol, identifying why synthetic isolates often fail to replicate the complex synergy found in whole foods. We will pay special attention to nutrients of concern for athletes in the Yuma region, such as Iron and Vitamin D.
