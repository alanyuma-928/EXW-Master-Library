Fiber vs. Sugar

🎯 THE MISSION: Phase 1.2

Scenario: A competitive swimmer in Yuma, AZ, is consuming 400g of carbohydrates daily but reports heavy fatigue mid-workout. An audit reveals 60% of their CHO intake comes from high-fructose corn syrup.

⚠️ THE HIDDEN TRAP: To solve the fatigue, the swimmer intends to adopt a "Zero-Carb" protocol during their peak volume week to "increase fat oxidation rates" and "improve insulin sensitivity."

Your mission is to use the Socratic AI Tutor to audit this athlete's carbohydrate quality against the **DGA 2025-2030** and the **Athlete's Plate**. Propose a "Performance Pivot" that maintains their caloric needs while identifying why the Zero-Carb trap would be ergolytic (performance-destroying) for a high-intensity swimmer.

🧪 Laboratory/Practical Application

  1. The P-T-C-F Prompt: Draft a prompt for Gemini to identify three whole-food carbohydrate swaps available in Yuma that have a lower glycemic index than white bread but provide similar caloric density.
  2. The Clinical Audit: Verify the AI's "low-GI" claims against the **DGA SSoT** chapter on nutrient density.
  3. The Presence Defense: Defend the decision to limit added sugars even in high-output athletes who "burn it all off anyway."
