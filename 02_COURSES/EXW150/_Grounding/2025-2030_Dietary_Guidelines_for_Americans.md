# 2025-2030 Dietary Guidelines for Americans (Summary)

## 🎯 Key Principles

The Dietary Guidelines for Americans provide evidence-based recommendations to promote health, prevent chronic disease, and help people reach and maintain a healthy weight. These guidelines are designed for healthy Americans ages 2 years and older, including those at increased risk of chronic disease.

## 🍎 Core Recommendations

1.  **Follow a Healthy Dietary Pattern at Every Life Stage:** Nutrient needs vary across the lifespan. The guidelines emphasize meeting nutrient needs primarily from nutrient-dense foods and beverages.
2.  **Customize and Enjoy Nutrient-Dense Food and Beverage Choices to Reflect Personal Preferences, Cultural Traditions, and Budgetary Considerations:** All food and beverage choices matter. Make every bite count by shifting to nutrient-dense options.
3.  **Focus on Meeting Food Group Needs with Nutrient-Dense Foods and Beverages, and Stay Within Calorie Limits:**
    *   **Vegetables:** Vary your vegetables.
    *   **Fruits:** Focus on whole fruits.
    *   **Grains:** Make half your grains whole grains.
    *   **Dairy:** Move to low-fat or fat-free dairy milk or yogurt.
    *   **Protein Foods:** Vary your protein routine.
    *   **Oils:** Use healthy oils.
4.  **Limit Foods and Beverages Higher In Added Sugars, Saturated Fat, and Sodium, and Limit Alcoholic Beverages:** These components should be limited to small amounts, as they can contribute to overweight, obesity, and other chronic health conditions.

## 🥦 Healthy Dietary Patterns Include:

*   **Vegetables:** All types, including dark green, red and orange, legumes (beans and peas), starchy, and other vegetables.
*   **Fruits:** Especially whole fruits.
*   **Grains:** At least half of which are whole grains.
*   **Dairy:** Fat-free or low-fat milk, yogurt, cheese, and/or fortified soy beverages.
*   **Protein Foods:** Lean meats, poultry, and eggs; seafood; beans, peas, and lentils; and nuts, seeds, and soy products.
*   **Oils:** Vegetable oils and oils in food, such as seafood and nuts.

## ⚠️ Limits on Certain Components:

*   **Added Sugars:** Less than 10% of daily calories.
*   **Saturated Fat:** Less than 10% of daily calories.
*   **Sodium:** Less than 2,300 milligrams (mg) per day.
*   **Alcoholic Beverages:** In moderation—up to 1 drink per day for women and up to 2 drinks per day for men, and only by adults of legal drinking age.
